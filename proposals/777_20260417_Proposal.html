Welcome to Shanghai, China's most cosmopolitan city! After your international flight, your guide will meet you at the airport and escort you to your hotel. Today is dedicated to rest and recovery, allowing you to adjust to the local time and prepare for the exciting days ahead.

Your China journey begins in Shanghai, a city that seamlessly blends Eastern and Western cultures. Upon arrival, your private guide will greet you at the arrival hall and transfer you to your hotel. Take this day to rest and acclimate. Shanghai offers a perfect introduction to China's energy and diversity—you'll explore its charms tomorrow.

Day 2
Shanghai

Wukang Road – Stroll through the leafy streets of the former French Concession, lined with plane trees and Art Deco villas. The iconic Wukang Building, a 1924 apartment block designed by Hungarian architect László Hudec, is a must-see landmark
Nanjing Road – Walk along China's premier shopping street, bustling with energy, historic department stores, and neon lights

Begin your Shanghai exploration in the former French Concession with a walk along Wukang Road. The French Concession is Shanghai at its most atmospheric: plane trees shade quiet lanes lined with 1920s-30s villas and apartment buildings. The star attraction is the Wukang Building, a red-brick, ship-shaped apartment building designed by László Hudec in 1924. As you walk, your guide will share stories of the writers, artists, and revolutionaries who once called this neighborhood home. Next, head to Nanjing Road, Shanghai's premier shopping thoroughfare. This pedestrianized street has been the heart of Shanghai commerce for over a century, lined with flagship stores, historic department stores, and countless shops. The energy here is infectious—street performers, neon signs, and crowds create a vibrant urban spectacle. After lunch, venture to Tianzifang, hidden within the former French Concession's shikumen alleyways. Unlike the polished Xintiandi, Tianzifang retains a more organic, bohemian character. Narrow lanes branch into smaller alleys, revealing unexpected treasures: a ceramics studio here, a craft beer bar there, a traditional paper-cutting artist working in a courtyard. This is a wonderful place for souvenir shopping and simply getting lost in Shanghai's creative heart. As dusk falls, make your way to The Bund (Waitan), Shanghai's most famous waterfront promenade. The 1.5-kilometer stretch features 26 buildings in various architectural styles—Gothic, Baroque, Renaissance—that once housed banks and trading houses during Shanghai's golden age. Across the Huangpu River, the Pudong New Area presents a futuristic skyline dominated by the Oriental Pearl Tower, Shanghai Tower, and Shanghai World Financial Center. The view at sunset and into the evening is spectacular, as the colonial buildings glow with warm amber light while Pudong pulses with dynamic LED displays.

Tongli Water Town – Experience a quintessential Jiangnan water town with its network of canals, 49 ancient stone bridges, and well-preserved Ming and Qing architecture. A gondola-style boat ride offers the best perspective
Humble Administrator's Garden – Explore Suzhou's largest and most renowned classical garden, a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring exquisite pavilions, rockeries, and ponds
Suzhou Canal Cruise – Glide along the ancient Grand Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage site, to see Suzhou's traditional whitewashed houses and stone bridges from the water

After breakfast, drive to Suzhou, a city celebrated for its classical gardens, silk production, and intricate canal networks. Your first stop is Tongli Water Town, just 30 minutes from Suzhou's city center. This ancient town is crisscrossed by 15 canals and connected by 49 stone bridges, earning it the nickname "Venice of the East." Take a gondola-style boat ride through the narrow waterways, passing beneath arched bridges and alongside whitewashed houses. Visit the Retreat & Reflection Garden, another UNESCO-listed classical garden, and the Silk Museum to learn about Suzhou's renowned silk industry. After lunch, visit the Humble Administrator's Garden, a masterpiece of classical Chinese garden design spanning over five hectares. Built in 1513, the garden is divided into eastern, central, and western sections, each with its own character. The central section features the expansive Lotus Pond with its pavilions and bridges, while the western section offers intimate spaces with rockeries and waterfalls. The garden exemplifies the principles of classical Chinese landscape design: every rock, pond, and pavilion is arranged to create a harmonious microcosm of nature. In the evening, experience a Suzhou Canal Cruise along the ancient Grand Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage site that once served as a vital transportation artery connecting northern and southern China. The cruise offers a unique perspective on Suzhou's traditional architecture—whitewashed houses with black-tiled roofs lining the water, stone bridges arching overhead, and willow trees draping their branches into the canal. The boat glides through historic districts, with local musicians sometimes performing traditional Kunqu opera on the banks.

Lingshan Grand Buddha – Stand before the 88-meter bronze Buddha statue, one of the largest in the world. Climb the 218 steps to reach the base for a panoramic view of Taihu Lake
Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance – Witness the spectacular fountain show depicting the birth of Prince Siddhartha, where nine dragon statues spray water as the golden statue of the infant Buddha rotates
Brahma Palace – Explore the "Oriental Louvre," a magnificent modern structure blending Buddhist art, architecture, and culture. The palace features exquisite murals, carved ceilings, and a central dome with a breathtaking light display

Drive to Wuxi (approximately 1 hour) to visit the Lingshan Grand Buddha Scenic Area, a sprawling Buddhist cultural theme park at the foot of Longshan Mountain. Enter through the Five-Ming Bridge and walk along the Bodhi Avenue, lined with prayer flags and lotus-shaped lanterns. The first major attraction is the Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance, a spectacular fountain show that runs several times daily. When the performance begins, nine bronze dragon heads spray water toward a 7-meter-tall golden statue of the infant Buddha, which rotates to reveal all sides—a dramatic representation of the Buddhist legend. Continue to the Brahma Palace, an architectural marvel completed in 2008. The palace's interior is breathtaking: marble floors, hand-carved wooden ceilings, and a massive central dome decorated with Buddhist murals. The palace also houses the Great Enlightenment Hall, where the "Splendid Lingshan" performance combines dance, music, and projection mapping to tell the story of the Buddha's enlightenment. Finally, approach the 88-meter Grand Buddha. A 218-step staircase (symbolizing the 108 worldly desires) leads to the statue's base. Here, you can "embrace the Buddha's feet"—a traditional gesture for seeking blessings. The viewing platform offers sweeping views of Taihu Lake, the third-largest freshwater lake in China. After your visit, check into your hotel in Wuxi for the night.

Wuxi to Shangrao

Journey to Jiangxi province to discover Wangxian Valley, a stunning scenic area built on cliffs and described as a "fairyland town." As evening falls, the entire valley comes alive with spectacular lighting that transforms the waterfalls, cliffside buildings, and ancient bridges into a magical scene.

After breakfast, transfer to Wuxi Railway Station for a high-speed train to Shangrao, Jiangxi. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours, offering views of the changing landscape as you travel from the flat Jiangnan plains to the mountainous terrain of northeastern Jiangxi. Upon arrival at Shangrao Station, your local guide will transfer you to Wangxian Valley (about 1 hour). The valley is nestled deep in the mountains of Guangxin District and has gained fame for its dramatic cliffside architecture—traditional houses seemingly clinging to vertical rock faces, connected by winding stone paths and suspended bridges. Check into your hotel within the scenic area (recommended for the full experience). In the evening, explore the valley when it is most magical. The cliffside buildings are illuminated with warm amber lights, the waterfalls are lit from below, and the suspended bridges glow against the dark mountain backdrop. The reflection of these lights in the valley's streams creates a scene reminiscent of a Chinese fantasy painting. The Crane Bridge and Cascade Overlook are particularly beautiful after dark.

The walkway is designed to blend with the natural rock, creating the illusion of walking on air. Continue to Baizhu Cliff, one of the valley's highest viewpoints.
