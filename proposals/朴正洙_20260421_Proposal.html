Wukang Road — Stroll through the leafy streets of the former French Concession, lined with plane trees and Art Deco villas. The iconic Wukang Building, a 1924 apartment block designed by Hungarian architect László Hudec, is a must-see landmark.
Nanjing Road — Walk along China's premier shopping street, bustling with energy, historic department stores, and neon lights.
Tianzifang — Explore a labyrinthine arts and crafts enclave built within traditional shikumen (stone-gate) alleyways, offering a gritty yet charming mix of art studios, boutiques, and cafes.

Begin your Shanghai exploration in the former French Concession with a walk along Wukang Road. The French Concession is Shanghai at its most atmospheric: plane trees shade quiet lanes lined with 1920s-30s villas and apartment buildings. The star attraction is the Wukang Building, a red-brick, ship-shaped apartment building designed by László Hudec in 1924. As you walk, your guide will share stories of the writers, artists, and revolutionaries who once called this neighborhood home.

Next, head to Nanjing Road, Shanghai's premier shopping thoroughfare. This pedestrianized street has been the heart of Shanghai commerce for over a century, lined with flagship stores, historic department stores, and countless shops. The energy here is infectious—street performers, neon signs, and crowds create a vibrant urban spectacle.

After lunch, venture to Tianzifang, hidden within the former French Concession's shikumen alleyways. Unlike the polished Xintiandi, Tianzifang retains a more organic, bohemian character. Narrow lanes branch into smaller alleys, revealing unexpected treasures: a ceramics studio here, a craft beer bar there, a traditional paper-cutting artist working in a courtyard. This is a wonderful place for souvenir shopping and simply getting lost in Shanghai's creative heart.

As dusk falls, make your way to The Bund (Waitan), Shanghai's most famous waterfront promenade. The 1.5-kilometer stretch features 26 buildings in various architectural styles—Gothic, Baroque, Renaissance—that once housed banks and trading houses during Shanghai's golden age. Across the Huangpu River, the Pudong New Area presents a futuristic skyline dominated by the Oriental Pearl Tower, Shanghai Tower, and Shanghai World Financial Center. The view at sunset and into the evening is spectacular, as the colonial buildings glow with warm amber light while Pudong pulses with dynamic LED displays.

Day 3
Shanghai to Suzhou | Water Towns & Classical Gardens
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Drive to Suzhou (approx. 1.5 hours) → Tongli Water Town (boat ride) → Lunch → Humble Administrator's Garden → Suzhou Canal Cruise → Check into Suzhou hotel

🏨 HotelSuzhou hotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Tongli Water Town — Experience a quintessential Jiangnan water town with its network of canals, 49 ancient stone bridges, and well-preserved Ming and Qing architecture. A gondola-style boat ride offers the best perspective.
Humble Administrator's Garden — Explore Suzhou's largest and most renowned classical garden, a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring exquisite pavilions, rockeries, and ponds.
Suzhou Canal Cruise — Glide along the ancient Grand Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage site, to see Suzhou's traditional whitewashed houses and stone bridges from the water.

After breakfast, drive to Suzhou, a city celebrated for its classical gardens, silk production, and intricate canal networks. Your first stop is Tongli Water Town, just 30 minutes from Suzhou's city center. This ancient town is crisscrossed by 15 canals and connected by 49 stone bridges, earning it the nickname "Venice of the East." Take a gondola-style boat ride through the narrow waterways, passing beneath arched bridges and alongside whitewashed houses. Visit the Retreat & Reflection Garden, another UNESCO-listed classical garden, and the Silk Museum to learn about Suzhou's renowned silk industry.

After lunch, visit the Humble Administrator's Garden, a masterpiece of classical Chinese garden design spanning over five hectares. Built in 1513, the garden is divided into eastern, central, and western sections, each with its own character. The central section features the expansive Lotus Pond with its pavilions and bridges, while the western section offers intimate spaces with rockeries and waterfalls. The garden exemplifies the principles of classical Chinese landscape design: every rock, pond, and pavilion is arranged to create a harmonious microcosm of nature.

In the evening, experience a Suzhou Canal Cruise along the ancient Grand Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage site that once served as a vital transportation artery connecting northern and southern China. The cruise offers a unique perspective on Suzhou's traditional architecture—whitewashed houses with black-tiled roofs lining the water, stone bridges arching overhead, and willow trees draping their branches into the canal. The boat glides through historic districts, with local musicians sometimes performing traditional Kunqu opera on the banks.

Lingshan Grand Buddha — Stand before the 88-meter bronze Buddha statue, one of the largest in the world. Climb the 218 steps to reach the base for a panoramic view of Taihu Lake.
Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance — Witness the spectacular fountain show depicting the birth of Prince Siddhartha, where nine dragon statues spray water as the golden statue of the infant Buddha rotates.
Brahma Palace — Explore the "Oriental Louvre," a magnificent modern structure blending Buddhist art, architecture, and culture. The palace features exquisite murals, carved ceilings, and a central dome with a breathtaking light display.

Drive to Wuxi (approximately 1 hour) to visit the Lingshan Grand Buddha Scenic Area, a sprawling Buddhist cultural theme park at the foot of Longshan Mountain.

Enter through the Five-Ming Bridge and walk along the Bodhi Avenue, lined with prayer flags and lotus-shaped lanterns. The first major attraction is the Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance, a spectacular fountain show that runs several times daily. When the performance begins, nine bronze dragon heads spray water toward a 7-meter-tall golden statue of the infant Buddha, which rotates to reveal all sides—a dramatic representation of the Buddhist legend.

Continue to the Brahma Palace, an architectural marvel completed in 2008. The palace's interior is breathtaking: marble floors, hand-carved wooden ceilings, and a massive central dome decorated with Buddhist murals. The palace also houses the Great Enlightenment Hall, where the "Splendid Lingshan" performance combines dance, music, and projection mapping to tell the story of the Buddha's enlightenment.

Finally, approach the 88-meter Grand Buddha. A 218-step staircase (symbolizing the 108 worldly desires) leads to the statue's base. Here, you can "embrace the Buddha's feet"—a traditional gesture for seeking blessings. The viewing platform offers sweeping views of Taihu Lake, the third-largest freshwater lake in China. After your visit, check into your hotel in Wuxi for the night.

Huangshizhai — Ascend via cable car to one of Zhangjiajie's classic viewing platforms, offering panoramic views of the park's quartz-sandstone pillars. The "No.1 Natural Artwork" platform provides a sweeping vista of thousands of peaks.
Golden Whip Stream — Walk along one of Zhangjiajie's most serene trails, following a crystal-clear stream that winds through a lush canyon. The 5.7-kilometer path offers encounters with wildlife, unusual rock formations, and the peaceful beauty of the forest floor.

Today you enter Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, China's first national forest park and a UNESCO World Heritage site. The park's quartz-sandstone pillars, some reaching heights of 800 meters, create a landscape of surreal beauty.

Begin at Huangshizhai, one of the park's classic scenic areas. Take the cable car to the summit, where a relatively flat walking circuit offers multiple viewing platforms. The No.1 Natural Artwork platform provides a panoramic vista of thousands of pillars rising from the valley floor—a view that has made Zhangjiajie famous. Other notable viewpoints include the Star Platform and the Six Wonders Pavilion. The area is also home to the Herb Picking Slope, where legend says a Han Dynasty physician once collected rare medicinal plants from the cliff faces.

In the afternoon, descend to Golden Whip Stream (Jinbianxi), a gentle walking trail that follows a clear stream through a narrow canyon. The 5.7-kilometer path is mostly flat, making it accessible to most fitness levels. As you walk, you'll pass unusual rock formations with evocative names: the Golden Whip Rock (a towering pillar beside the stream), the Water Washing the Heart waterfall, and the Mother and Son peaks. The stream is home to a population of giant salamanders (Andrias davidianus)—protected amphibians that can grow to over a meter in length—though they are rarely seen. The trail is shaded by dense forest, with occasional breaks where the stream pools into emerald ponds. This is Zhangjiajie at its most tranquil.

Avatar Hallelujah Mountain — Stand before the 1,074-meter pillar that inspired the floating mountains in James Cameron's Avatar. The Southern Sky Column was officially renamed "Hallelujah Mountain" in 2010 following the film's global success.
Bailong Elevator — Ride the world's tallest outdoor elevator (326 meters), built directly into a cliff face. The 66-second ascent offers spectacular views as you emerge from the rock.
Tianzi Mountain — Known as the "King of Peak Forests," this area offers panoramic views of thousands of stone pillars rising from the mist.
Ten-Mile Gallery — Admire the natural rock formations lining a picturesque valley, best viewed from the sightseeing train.

Today explores the heart of the Wulingyuan Scenic Area, including the sections made famous by the film Avatar.

Begin with the Bailong Elevator, an engineering marvel that transports visitors from the valley floor to the heights of Yuanjiajie in just 66 seconds. As you ascend, watch the cliff face slide past and the view expand into a sea of pillars.

At Yuanjiajie, the highlight is Hallelujah Mountain (Southern Sky Column), a 1,074-meter pillar that served as the primary visual reference for the film's floating Hallelujah Mountains. Nearby, walk across the First Bridge Under Heaven, a natural stone arch connecting two peaks with a sheer drop of 350 meters beneath your feet. The Fascination Terrace offers multiple viewing platforms where pillars seem to float in a sea of green.

After lunch, visit Tianzi Mountain, accessible via cable car. The area's name means "Son of Heaven Mountain," commemorating Xiang Dakun, a local farmer who led a peasant uprising in the 14th century and declared himself "Son of Heaven." From viewing platforms like He Long Park and West Sea, you'll gaze upon a seemingly endless array of stone pillars shrouded in mist. The Imperial Writing Brush Peak and Fairy Presenting Flowers are among the formations named for their resemblance to traditional Chinese motifs.

Conclude at Ten-Mile Gallery, where a series of peaks line a picturesque valley. You can take the sightseeing train (optional) to comfortably view formations named for their shapes: "Old Man Collecting Herbs," "Sisters Peak," and "Fingers of the Buddha." The gentle stroll or train ride provides a relaxing conclusion to a day of dramatic mountain scenery.

Today you explore Tianmen Mountain, Zhangjiajie's iconic city-side peak, before traveling to Chongqing.

The adventure begins with the Tianmen Mountain Cable Car—one of the world's longest, rising directly from the city center to the summit. During the 30-minute journey, you'll watch the urban landscape shrink beneath you as you ascend into the clouds, with the famous 99-bend Tongtian Avenue (Road to Heaven) winding like a silver ribbon far below.

At the summit, follow the West Line trail. The Glass Skywalk is a 60-meter walkway suspended against a vertical cliff face with a transparent floor revealing the valley far below. A series of mountain-penetrating escalators carry you down through the mountain's interior. You'll emerge at Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate), a natural arch 131.5 meters high and 57 meters wide. According to historical records, the cave formed in 263 AD when an earthquake split the mountain open. From the cave, you may descend the 999 steps to the base—in Chinese tradition, the number 9 symbolizes eternity, and climbing these stairs is believed to bring blessings. Liziba Viewing Platform — Witness one of Chongqing's most famous urban oddities—a light rail train that passes directly through a residential building. The viewing platform offers the perfect vantage point for photos.
Eling No.2 Factory — Explore a repurposed industrial site transformed into a creative hub, offering art galleries, trendy cafes, and panoramic views of the Yangtze River.

Chongqing's unique topography has produced some extraordinary urban solutions, and Liziba Light Rail Station is perhaps the most famous. Visit the Liziba Viewing Platform, which offers the perfect vantage point to watch Line 2 of the city's light rail system pass directly through a residential building—not above it or beside it, but through the middle. Chengdu
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Chengdu Panda Base → Lunch → Sanxingdui Museum → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Chengdu Panda Base — Observe giant pandas in a carefully designed naturalistic habitat at this world-renowned research and conservation facility. See pandas of all ages, from tiny cubs to adults, as they eat, play, and rest.
Sanxingdui Museum — Journey back 3,000 years to explore the mysterious Bronze Age civilization of the ancient Shu kingdom, featuring extraordinary artifacts—including towering bronze masks and golden scepters—that challenge conventional understanding of Chinese civilization.

Today focuses on two of Chengdu's most remarkable attractions, spanning thousands of years of history and culture.

Begin at the Chengdu Panda Base, a research and conservation facility dedicated to the protection of giant pandas and red pandas. Unlike a traditional zoo, the panda base provides a naturalistic environment where pandas roam in spacious enclosures with bamboo forests, climbing structures, and pools. The best viewing times are in the morning (around 8:00-10:00 AM) when the pandas are most active—eating bamboo, playing with each other, and occasionally wrestling. You'll see pandas of all ages, including adorable cubs in the nursery (seasonal). The facility also offers educational exhibits on conservation efforts to protect these beloved animals, whose wild population has slowly recovered thanks to dedicated work.

After lunch, venture to Sanxingdui Museum, one of China's most significant archaeological sites. Discovered in 1986, Sanxingdui revealed a previously unknown Bronze Age civilization that flourished in the Sichuan basin over 3,000 years ago, contemporaneous with the Shang dynasty of central China but with a completely distinct culture. The artifacts are astonishing: giant bronze masks with protruding eyes, towering bronze trees, gold scepters, and jade objects that bear no resemblance to any other Chinese civilization. These discoveries have led scholars to reconsider narratives about the origins of Chinese civilization. The museum's modern building and thoughtfully presented exhibits make it an unforgettable journey into a lost world.

Chengdu

🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Dujiangyan (approx. 1 hour) → Dujiangyan Irrigation System exploration → Lunch → Return to Chengdu → People's Park (Heming Teahouse) → Chunxi Road → Taikoo Li → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Dujiangyan Irrigation System — Marvel at a 2,200-year-old engineering wonder that still functions today. This UNESCO World Heritage site tamed the Min River and made the Chengdu Plain "the Land of Abundance."
People's Park & Heming Teahouse — Experience everyday Chengdu life at this beloved urban oasis, where locals gather at the historic Heming Teahouse for tea, mahjong, and conversation.
Chunxi Road & Taikoo Li — Experience Chengdu's modern urban energy, from the bustling pedestrian shopping street to the sophisticated open-air complex that artfully blends contemporary architecture with preserved historic buildings.

Drive approximately one hour to Dujiangyan, a small city famous for its ancient irrigation system. Built around 256 BC during the Warring States period, the Dujiangyan Irrigation System is the world's oldest and only surviving no-dam irrigation project. Designed by Li Bing and his son, it cleverly uses natural topography to divert water from the Min River, control flooding, and irrigate the Chengdu Plain—transforming the region into one of China's most productive agricultural areas. The system still functions today, a testament to ancient Chinese engineering wisdom.

The site includes several key features: the Yuzui (Fish Mouth) levee that splits the river, the Feishayan (Flying Sand Weir) that controls floodwaters, and the Baopingkou (Bottle-Neck Channel) that regulates flow into the irrigation network. Walk across the ancient Anlan Suspension Bridge, explore temples dedicated to Li Bing, and appreciate the integration of human ingenuity with natural landscapes.

Return to Chengdu in the afternoon to experience the city's famous teahouse culture at People's Park (Renmin Gongyuan). The historic Heming Teahouse has been serving tea for over a century. Find a bamboo chair, order a cup of jasmine tea, and watch locals play mahjong, practice calligraphy, or simply enjoy the relaxed atmosphere that defines Chengdu.

In the evening, experience Chengdu's modern urban energy. Chunxi Road is Chengdu's premier shopping destination—a bustling pedestrianized street packed with flagship stores and department stores. Nearby, Taikoo Li offers a more sophisticated experience, masterfully preserving several historic buildings while surrounding them with sleek contemporary architecture and luxury boutiques.
