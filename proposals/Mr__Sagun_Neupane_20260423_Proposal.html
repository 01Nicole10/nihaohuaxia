Day 02
Day 2
Beijing · Imperial Heritage Tour
▾
🗺Tiananmen Square → Forbidden City (Palace Museum) → Temple of Heaven
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotel4-star hotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tiananmen Square — Admire the majestic Tiananmen Rostrum, the Monument to the People’s Heroes, and the grandeur of the world’s largest public square
Forbidden City — Explore this UNESCO World Heritage Site, the imperial residence of 24 Ming and Qing emperors, featuring grand courtyards, exquisite architecture, and precious cultural relics
Temple of Heaven — Visit the sacred complex dedicated to imperial prayers for good harvests, renowned for its intricate design and perfect acoustic effects

Start the day with Tiananmen Square, the spiritual and political heart of China. Stand in awe of the Tiananmen Rostrum, where important national ceremonies are held, and the Monument to the People’s Heroes, a solemn tribute to those who sacrificed for the nation. The square offers a sweeping view of Beijing’s iconic landmarks, setting the tone for your imperial heritage tour.

Next, enter the Forbidden City, a sprawling complex covering 720,000 square meters, surrounded by a 10-meter-high wall and a 52-meter-wide moat. As you wander through its 980 buildings and 8,707 rooms, your guide will share stories of emperors, concubines, and court life, bringing the palace’s history to life. Marvel at the Hall of Supreme Harmony, the largest wooden structure in China, and the intricate details of the imperial gardens, which exemplify classical Chinese design.

After lunch, visit the Temple of Heaven, a masterpiece of ancient Chinese architecture located in the southern part of Beijing. This sacred complex was where emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties performed annual ceremonies to pray for abundant harvests. The Circular Mound Altar, the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests, and the Imperial Vault of Heaven showcase the harmony between man and nature in traditional Chinese culture, with their intricate designs and perfect acoustic effects leaving a lasting impression.

Day 3
Beijing · Great Wall
▾
🗺Great Wall → Photo stop at Bird’s Nest (National Stadium) → Return to hotel

Mutianyu Great Wall — Hike the most well-preserved and iconic section of the Great Wall, enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and feel the grandeur of ancient Chinese civilization
Bird’s Nest — A photo stop at this modern Beijing landmark, famous for its unique and innovative architectural design

After breakfast, board your private vehicle for a 1.5-hour drive to the Great Wall, the most famous and well-preserved section of this ancient defensive structure. As you approach, the sight of the wall winding over the mountains like a giant dragon will take your breath away. Hike along the winding battlements, climb the watchtowers, and enjoy panoramic views of the lush mountains stretching as far as the eye can see. This is the perfect place to understand why the Great Wall is regarded as one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

On the way back to the city, make a photo stop at the Bird’s Nest (National Stadium), a landmark of modern Beijing built for the 2008 Olympic Games. Its unique lattice-like design resembles a bird’s nest, making it one of the most recognizable buildings in the world. Capture a memorable photo of this architectural marvel before returning to your hotel for a rest.

Day 04
Day 4
Beijing → Xi'an · Summer Palace
▾
🗺Summer Palace → Transfer to Beijing West Railway Station → Xi’an
✈ TransportPrivate car, High-speed train
🏨 HotelHotel in Xi'an
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Summer Palace — Explore China’s largest and best-preserved imperial garden, featuring a stunning lake, lush greenery, and elegant pavilions
High-Speed Train Journey — Experience China’s world-class high-speed rail, traveling from Beijing to Xi'an in approximately 4.5 hours

Spend the morning exploring the Summer Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest imperial garden in China. Covering an area of 290 hectares, the garden is centered around Kunming Lake, which accounts for three-quarters of its total area. Stroll along the Long Corridor, a 728-meter-long covered walkway decorated with over 14,000 paintings, depicting scenes from Chinese history and mythology. Take a leisurely boat ride on Kunming Lake to enjoy the serene scenery, with the Longevity Hill and its elegant pavilions reflecting in the water.

After lunch, transfer to Beijing West Railway Station to board the high-speed train to Xi’an. China’s high-speed rail network is renowned for its speed, comfort, and punctuality, offering a smooth and enjoyable journey. As you travel westward, you will witness the landscape change from the flat North China Plain to the rolling hills of Shaanxi Province, providing a glimpse of China’s diverse geography.

Upon arrival in Xi'an, you will be transfer to hotel for rest, Xi’an, one of China’s ancient capitals, boasts a history of over 3,000 years and was once the capital of 13 dynasties. Settle in and rest, as tomorrow you will explore the city’s most famous attraction—the Terracotta Army.

Day 05
Day 5
Xi'an · Terracotta Warriors & Mini Clay Warrior Making
▾
🗺Terracotta Army Museum → Clay Warrior Making Experience → Muslim Quarter
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelHotel in Xi'an
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Terracotta Army Museum — Marvel at one of the world's greatest archaeological wonders: thousands of life-sized warriors, horses, and chariots standing in battle formation
Mini Clay Warrior Making Experience — Learn a traditional craft recognized as intangible cultural heritage, and create your own miniature terracotta warrior to take home
Muslim Quarter — Immerse yourself in the sights, sounds, and aromas of Xi'an's most vibrant food street, from sizzling lamb skewers to sweet persimmon cakes

After breakfast, you'll be transferred to the Terracotta Army Museum, located about 30 kilometers east of Xi'an. Discovered in 1974 by local farmers, this UNESCO World Heritage Site has since become one of China's most extraordinary attractions. The museum features three main pits containing over 8,000 life-sized terracotta soldiers, horses, and chariots — each figure with its own unique facial expression, hairstyle, and clothing, reflecting the incredible diversity of the Qin Dynasty army.

Your guide will lead you through the pits, sharing the fascinating story of Emperor Qin Shi Huang, who commissioned this entire army to accompany him into the afterlife. You'll have the chance to admire the intricate details: the armor plates on the soldiers, the muscular definition of the horses, and the spokes of the chariot wheels — all showcasing the remarkable craftsmanship of ancient China. The sight of thousands of warriors standing in disciplined ranks is truly awe-inspiring and stands as a powerful testament to China's early civilization.

Want to truly experience the artistry? In the afternoon, you'll learn the delicate craft of making miniature terracotta warriors at a local workshop. Under the guidance of skilled artisans, you'll shape your own small warrior — and take your handcrafted piece home as a cherished souvenir.

After returning to downtown Xi'an, you'll visit the Muslim Quarter. Follow your nose through narrow alleys lined with food stalls offering local favorites like yangrou paomo (crumbled flatbread in mutton stew), sizzling lamb skewers, and sweet persimmon cakes. It's the perfect place to end your day, tasting your way through Xi'an's rich culinary heritage.

Day 6
Xi'an → Shanghai · Ancient City
▾
🗺Xi’an Ancient City Wall → Xi‘an Museum & Small Wild Goose Pagoda → Flight to Zhangjiajie
✈ TransportPrivate car, Flight
🏨 HotelHotel in Shanghai
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Xi’an Museum & Small Wild Goose Pagoda — Explore Xi‘an’s 3,000-year history through exquisite artifacts, then admire the elegant Tang‑era pagoda that has stood for over 1,300 years
Xi’an Ancient City Wall — Walk or cycle atop one of the best‑preserved ancient city walls in the world, offering sweeping views of old and new Xi‘an

In the morning, you’ll head to the Xi‘an Ancient City Wall — the largest and best-preserved city wall in China. Stretching nearly 14 kilometers around the old city, it offers a fantastic perspective of Xi’an. You can choose to walk along the wide ramparts, rent a bicycle (the most popular way to cover the full loop in about 1.5 hours), or simply take in the views of the Bell Tower and the modern skyline beyond. The contrast between the ancient brickwork and the bustling city below is a perfect metaphor for Xi‘an itself — a place where history lives alongside the present.

Then, you’ll visit the Xi‘an Museum and the Small Wild Goose Pagoda. The museum houses a rich collection spanning from the Zhou to the Tang dynasties, including bronze ware, gold and silver artifacts, and ancient calligraphy. Right on the museum grounds stands the Small Wild Goose Pagoda — a beautifully preserved brick pagoda built during the Tang Dynasty (circa 707 AD). Originally containing Buddhist scriptures brought back from India, the pagoda survived multiple earthquakes and still leans slightly today, adding to its charm. Take a leisurely stroll through the peaceful courtyard, and if you’re lucky, you may hear the famous “Morning Bell of the Small Wild Goose Pagoda” — a melodic chime that once marked the passage of time for ancient Chang‘an.

Finally, you’ll be transferred to the airport/train to Shanghai.

After lunch, stroll along The Bund (Waitan), Shanghai’s most famous waterfront promenade. Day 08
Shanghai · Zhujiajiao Ancient Water Town
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Zhujiajiao Ancient Town (approx. 1 hour) → Explore Zhujiajiao: Fangsheng Bridge, Kezhi Garden, canal boat ride → Lunch → Return to Shanghai → Free time → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Zhujiajiao Ancient Town — Escape to a 1,700-year-old water town on the outskirts of Shanghai, often called the “Venice of Shanghai.”
Fangsheng Bridge — Cross the largest and most famous stone arch bridge in Zhujiajiao, dating back to 1571, offering panoramic views of the town’s canals and rooftops.
Kezhi Garden — Visit a classical Jiangnan-style garden built in 1912, featuring exquisite pavilions, rockeries, and a tranquil lotus pond.
Canal Boat Ride — Glide through the narrow waterways in a traditional gondola-style boat for the best perspective on the town’s ancient architecture.

Today you escape the city to visit Zhujiajiao Ancient Town, one of Shanghai’s best-preserved water towns with a history spanning over 1,700 years. Located on the banks of Dianshan Lake, Zhujiajiao is often called the “Venice of Shanghai” for its intricate network of canals and ancient bridges.

Begin at Fangsheng Bridge, the largest and most famous stone arch bridge in the town. Built in 1571 during the Ming Dynasty, the bridge features elegant arches and offers panoramic views of the canals, traditional houses, and willow trees lining the waterways.

Visit Kezhi Garden, a classical Jiangnan-style garden built in 1912. The garden combines elements of traditional Chinese garden design with Western architectural influences, featuring exquisite pavilions, rockeries, a lotus pond, and winding corridors.

Take a canal boat ride through the narrow waterways, passing beneath ancient stone bridges and alongside whitewashed houses with black-tiled roofs. From the water, you’ll see local residents going about their daily lives—washing clothes on stone steps, hanging laundry from wooden balconies, and tending small gardens.

Explore the town‘s ancient streets, lined with traditional shops selling local specialties: zongzi (sticky rice dumplings), zhajiangmian (noodles with soybean paste), and various snacks. The North Street is particularly famous for its preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

After lunch, return to Shanghai. The remainder of the day is free for relaxation or last-minute exploration.
