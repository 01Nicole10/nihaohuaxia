Dear Sherelene Kennedy
Thank You for Your Trust

Dear Sherelene, welcome to your bespoke cultural odyssey across China — where mist-shrouded sandstone pillars, Ming-dynasty ramparts, Tang-era temples, and cosmopolitan heritage lanes await. Your 12-day journey in November 2026 is thoughtfully paced to immerse you and your travel companion in layered history, from the primordial landscapes of Zhangjiajie to the imperial grandeur of Beijing and Xi’an, capped by Shanghai’s fusion of colonial elegance and revolutionary legacy.

Day by Day
Your Detailed Itinerary
Day 01
Zhangjiajie — Avatar Mountains
Ancient Peaks & Glass Bridge
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (Golden Whip Stream & Avatar Hallelujah Mountain) → Lunch at local Tujia restaurant → Tianzi Mountain Nature Reserve → Hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelZhangjiajie Furama Resort
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Golden Whip Stream — scenic 2-hour riverside walk beneath towering quartzite sandstone pillars (2 hrs) | Avatar Hallelujah Mountain — iconic floating peak inspiration for James Cameron’s film (45 mins) | Tianzi Mountain — panoramic sunrise views over sea of clouds and stone forests (1.5 hrs) | Bailong Elevator — world’s tallest outdoor glass elevator ascending 326m through cliffside (20 mins)

Begin your journey amid the surreal karst formations that inspired Pandora — a landscape shaped over 380 million years and revered in Daoist tradition as a realm of immortals. Your private guide will reveal how these mountains feature in ancient Chinese poetry and local Tujia folklore, making every trail a passage through living myth.

Day 02
Zhangjiajie — Canyon Majesty & Cultural Immersion
Grand Canyon & Tujia Heritage
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon (Glass Bridge & Emerald Pool) → Lunch → Baofeng Lake (dragon boat cruise & cliffside caves) → Evening Tujia folk performance
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelZhangjiajie Furama Resort
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge — 430m-long transparent skywalk suspended 300m above canyon floor (1.5 hrs) | Emerald Pool — jade-green alpine lake surrounded by ancient cypress and inscribed Song-dynasty steles (45 mins) | Baofeng Lake — tranquil 2km dragon boat cruise past limestone grottoes and Han dynasty cliff carvings (1 hr) | Tujia Minority Performance — drum dances, hand-waving songs, and silk-embroidery demonstrations (1 hr)

Today deepens your connection with both nature’s drama and humanity’s enduring presence — walking the world’s longest glass bridge, gliding past millennia-old cave sanctuaries, and witnessing centuries-old Tujia traditions performed under lantern light. This is history not observed behind glass, but felt in rhythm and reverence.

Day 03
Beijing — Forbidden City & Imperial Heart
Ming Palaces & Hutong Whispers
▾
🗺Early flight Zhangjiajie → Beijing → Airport transfer → Check-in → Afternoon Forbidden City tour → Evening stroll through Nanluoguxiang Hutong
✈ TransportDomestic flight + Private car
🏨 HotelThe Peninsula Beijing
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Dinner
Forbidden City — guided exploration of the 9,999-room imperial palace complex, including Hall of Supreme Harmony and Palace of Heavenly Purity (3 hrs) | Meridian Gate — ceremonial entrance used only by emperors; learn Ming-Qing court protocols (30 mins) | Nanluoguxiang — 700-year-old alleyway with Ming-era courtyard residences and scholar’s gardens (1.5 hrs) | Houhai Lakeside — sunset view of Drum Tower and historic lakeshore teahouses (45 mins)

Step into the epicenter of imperial power where emperors ruled for nearly 500 years. Your expert historian guide deciphers symbolism in roof ornaments, color-coded gates, and astronomical alignments — transforming stone, timber, and tile into vivid narratives of ceremony, conspiracy, and celestial order.

Day 04
Beijing — Great Wall & Temple Serenity
Mountain Ramparts & Buddhist Wisdom
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Mutianyu Great Wall (cable car up, toboggan down) → Lunch → Temple of Heaven (Imperial Sacrificial Altar & Echo Wall) → Evening Peking Opera introduction
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelThe Peninsula Beijing
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Mutianyu Great Wall — well-preserved 15th-century section with 22 watchtowers; cable car ascent and toboggan descent (3 hrs) | Temple of Heaven — walk the Circular Mound Altar where emperors prayed for bountiful harvests (1.5 hrs) | Echo Wall — whisper across 65m along glazed brick corridor demonstrating Ming acoustical mastery (20 mins) | Peking Opera Workshop — mask painting, basic gesture demonstration, and aria listening session (1 hr)

Feel the weight of dynastic ambition atop the sinuous Mutianyu ramparts, then experience the humility of imperial devotion at the Temple of Heaven — where architecture mirrored cosmic harmony. Your evening ends with the stylized elegance of Peking Opera, a UNESCO-recognized art form born in Qing court circles.

Day 05
Beijing — Scholar’s Legacy & Revolutionary Memory
Summer Palaces & Red History
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Summer Palace (Kunming Lake & Longevity Hill) → Lunch → National Museum of China (Ancient China & Revolution Galleries) → Farewell dinner at Liang Yuan Courtyard Restaurant
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelThe Peninsula Beijing
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Summer Palace — imperial garden retreat built by Empress Dowager Cixi; visit Marble Boat and Seventeen-Arch Bridge (2.5 hrs) | Wenchang Pavilion — Ming-era Confucian study pavilion overlooking Kunming Lake (30 mins) | National Museum of China — curated highlights from Shang bronzes to May Fourth Movement artifacts (2 hrs) | Liang Yuan Courtyard — Ming-dynasty residence turned fine-dining venue serving imperial banquet-inspired cuisine (1 hr)

From the poetic restraint of scholar-gardens to the sweeping narrative of China’s modern awakening, today bridges contemplation and transformation. You’ll trace Confucian ideals in pavilion inscriptions and witness how revolution redefined national identity — all within the same city’s storied walls.

Day 06
Xi’an — Terracotta Warriors & Tang Splendor
Emperor’s Army & Silk Road Grandeur
▾
🗺Morning flight Beijing → Xi’an → Transfer to hotel → Afternoon Terracotta Army Museum → Evening Tang Dynasty Music & Dance Show
✈ TransportDomestic flight + Private car
🏨 HotelThe St. Regis Xi’an
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Dinner
Terracotta Army Pit 1 — vast excavation site with over 2,000 life-sized warriors, each with unique facial features (2 hrs) | Bronze Chariots Exhibition — two 2,200-year-old half-scale imperial chariots with movable parts and gold-silver inlay (45 mins) | Hanyangling Mausoleum — lesser-known Western Han tomb complex with miniature terracotta figures and ritual pottery (1 hr) | Tang Dynasty Show — live orchestral music, silk costumes, and choreography reconstructed from Dunhuang murals (1.5 hrs)

Stand before Qin Shi Huang’s immortal army — a funerary masterpiece revealing astonishing craftsmanship, military organization, and spiritual belief. Later, let the Tang Dynasty’s golden age come alive through music and movement that echo Silk Road exchanges with Persia, India, and Central Asia.

Day 07
Xi’an — Ancient Walls & Muslim Quarter
Ming Ramparts & Scented Bazaars
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Xi’an City Wall (bike ride on 14th-century ramparts) → Lunch → Small Wild Goose Pagoda & Jianfu Temple → Evening exploration of Muslim Quarter street food and calligraphy stalls
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelThe St. Regis Xi’an
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Xi’an City Wall — bike along the world’s best-preserved Ming fortification (13.7km), stopping at South Gate and Bell Tower views (1.5 hrs) | Small Wild Goose Pagoda — 8th-century Buddhist pagoda built during Tang era; part of UNESCO-listed ‘Silk Roads’ site (45 mins) | Jianfu Temple — original Tang monastery grounds with rare Song-dynasty bell tower and sutra library (30 mins) | Muslim Quarter — taste persimmon cakes, roujiamo, and hand-pulled biangbiang noodles while watching ink-brush masters (1.5 hrs)

Pedal atop history — the very ramparts that defended Chang’an, capital of 13 dynasties and the largest city on earth in the 8th century. Then wander alleys where Arabic calligraphy meets Mandarin signs, a testament to Xi’an’s role as the eastern terminus of the Silk Road and cradle of interfaith dialogue.

Day 08
Xi’an — Han Tombs & Calligraphic Soul
Scholar Stones & Underground Palaces
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Shaanxi History Museum (pre-booked priority access) → Lunch → Stele Forest Museum (epigraphic treasures from Han to Qing) → Optional calligraphy workshop
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelThe St. Regis Xi’an
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Shaanxi History Museum — world-class collection: Han jade burial suits, Tang tri-color glazed pottery horses, and Tang dynasty gold hairpins (2 hrs) | Stele Forest Museum — 3,000+ stone steles including Yan Zhenqing’s ‘Yan Family Temple Stele’ and the Nestorian Stele (2 hrs) | Forest of Stone Steles — oldest extant Confucian classic texts carved in Song dynasty (45 mins) | Calligraphy Workshop — write your name in seal script using traditional inkstone and wolf-hair brush (1 hr)

Delve into China’s written soul — where philosophy, poetry, and politics were etched in stone for eternity. From Han dynasty jade armor meant to preserve the emperor’s qi to Tang calligraphy that shaped East Asian aesthetics, this day reveals how literacy, memory, and power converged in ink and granite.

Day 09
Shanghai — Colonial Echoes & Revolutionary Roots
Bund Grandeur & French Concession Charm
▾
🗺Morning flight Xi’an → Shanghai → Transfer to hotel → Afternoon The Bund & Waibaidu Bridge → Lunch → French Concession walking tour (Shikumen lanes & former residences of Zhou Enlai & Soong sisters) → Evening Huangpu River cruise
✈ TransportDomestic flight + Private car
🏨 HotelThe Langham, Shanghai, Xintiandi
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
The Bund — stroll iconic waterfront promenade with 52 historic buildings spanning Neo-Classical to Art Deco (1.5 hrs) | Waibaidu Bridge — 1907 steel truss bridge, symbol of Shanghai’s treaty-port era (20 mins) | Shikumen Houses — explore 1920s stone-gate residences blending Jiangnan courtyard design with Western brickwork (1 hr) | Former Site of the First CPC National Congress — birthplace of the Chinese Communist Party, preserved 1920s shikumen compound (45 mins)

Witness Shanghai’s layered identity — where British banks face Pudong’s futuristic skyline, and revolutionary meetings unfolded in quiet lilong lanes. Your guide shares stories of Sun Yat-sen’s exile, Soong Ching-ling’s diplomacy, and how architecture encoded shifting ideologies across a century.

Day 10
Shanghai — Jiangnan Gardens & Modern Art
Scholar Refinement & Avant-Garde Visions
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Yu Garden & Chenghuang Temple Complex → Lunch → Shanghai Museum (Ancient Chinese Art Galleries) → Evening free at Xintiandi
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelThe Langham, Shanghai, Xintiandi
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Yu Garden — 16th-century Ming dynasty scholar’s garden with zigzag bridges, rockeries, and lotus ponds symbolizing Daoist balance (1.5 hrs) | Chenghuang Temple — Taoist temple complex with Ming-era incense burners and opera stage (45 mins) | Shanghai Museum — unparalleled bronze, ceramics, calligraphy, and painting collections, including Song dynasty ‘Dwelling in the Fuchun Mountains’ scroll replica (2 hrs) | Xintiandi — adaptive reuse of 1920s shikumen blocks housing galleries, boutiques, and historic plaques (1 hr)

Transition from the meditative stillness of a Ming scholar’s garden — where every rock placement reflects philosophical intent — to the bold strokes of China’s contemporary art scene. At the Shanghai Museum, stand before Song dynasty masterpieces that defined East Asian aesthetics for a millennium.

Day 11
Shanghai — Water Town Whispers & Silk Legacy
Ancient Canals & Imperial Textiles
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Half-day visit to Zhujiajiao Water Town (‘Venice of Shanghai’) → Lunch → China Silk Museum (Hangzhou branch, accessible via express train + car) → Return to Shanghai
✈ TransportPrivate car + High-speed train (30-min ride)
🏨 HotelThe Langham, Shanghai, Xintiandi
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Zhujiajiao — 1,700-year-old canal town with Ming-Qing bridges, boat rides under stone arches, and folk textile workshops (2 hrs) | Kezhi Garden — 19th-century scholar’s garden with ‘Hall of One Hundred Steps’ and bamboo-lined courtyards (45 mins) | China Silk Museum — world’s largest silk museum; view Han dynasty brocade fragments and imperial Qing dragon robes (1.5 hrs) | Silk weaving demo — watch artisans operate 18th-century drawlooms replicating Tang patterns (45 mins)

Drift along canals where Song merchants traded silk and scholars composed poetry — then step into the museum that traces silk’s journey from Neolithic cocoon to imperial regalia and global commodity. This is material culture as living history: tactile, luminous, and deeply entwined with China’s civilizational reach.

Day 12
Shanghai — Departure & Last Reflections
Lingering Light & Farewell Tea
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Morning free for last-minute shopping or quiet reflection at Jing’an Temple → Transfer to PVG airport with farewell gift box (tea, calligraphy set, map journal)
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelThe Langham, Shanghai, Xintiandi
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Jing’an Temple — 1,700-year-old Buddhist temple rebuilt in Song style, nestled amid skyscrapers (45 mins) | Jing’an Park — peaceful lakeside stroll with ginkgo avenues and Ming-style pavilions (30 mins) | Farewell tea ceremony — traditional preparation of Longjing green tea with seasonal osmanthus (30 mins) | Gift box presentation — hand-selected premium teas, inkstone mini-set, and linen-bound itinerary journal (20 mins)

End your journey where ancient faith meets modern pulse — at a temple that survived dynastic collapse, war, and urban renewal. Over fragrant Longjing tea, reflect on twelve days that spanned 2,200 years of history, from terra-cotta soldiers to silk looms to Pudong skylines — all united by China’s enduring reverence for memory, mastery, and meaning.
