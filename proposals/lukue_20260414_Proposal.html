Day 01
Day 1: June 5
Guangzhou Arrival
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
Welcome to China! After your long journey from Houston via Vancouver, your guide will meet you at Hong Kong International Airport and escort you across the border to Guangzhou. Today is dedicated to rest and recovery, allowing you to adjust to the local time.

Your China journey begins as you arrive in Hong Kong. Your driver will greet you at the arrival hall and transfer you to Guangzhou (approximately 2-2.5 hours). Guangzhou, historically known as Canton, is the capital of Guangdong province and a major trading port for centuries. Upon checking into your hotel, take time to rest and prepare for the exciting days ahead.

Day 02
Day 2: June 6
Guangzhou | Lingnan Culture & Pearl River Night
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Chen Clan Academy — Explore a stunning example of traditional Lingnan architecture, built between 1890 and 1894 as a study hall for Chen family students preparing for imperial examinations. The complex now houses the Guangdong Folk Art Museum, featuring intricate wood, stone, and ceramic carvings.
Yuexiu Park — Visit Guangzhou’s largest park, home to the iconic Five Rams Statue—the symbol of the city—and the ancient Zhenhai Tower (Guangzhou Museum). The park covers 690,000 square meters with 2,200 years of city history.
Shamian Island — Stroll through a peaceful colonial-era enclave where European consulates, churches, and mansions from the 19th century stand amidst tree-lined boulevards.
Yongqingfang — Wander through a beautifully restored historical neighborhood where 1930s qilou (arcade-style buildings) blend with modern art galleries, boutique shops, and cafes.
Canton Tower (exterior) — View China‘s tallest tower (604 meters), an architectural marvel with a distinctive twisted design, illuminated at night.
Pearl River Night Cruise — Glide along the Pearl River as illuminated bridges and gleaming skyscrapers reflect in the water. The cruise covers the golden waters from Canton Tower to the Pearl River.

Begin at Chen Clan Academy, a magnificent complex representing the pinnacle of Lingnan artistry. The building features 19 buildings with nine halls and six courtyards connected in a symmetric pattern, topped with 11 ceramic ridge crests resembling dragons. Next, visit Yuexiu Park, a sprawling green oasis. The Five Rams Statue, carved from 130 granite blocks, commemorates the legend of five immortals who rode into Guangzhou on rams—giving the city its nickname “City of Rams.“ Climb Zhenhai Tower, a five-story structure built in 1380, for panoramic city views. Explore Shamian Island, a sandbar that became a British and French concession after the Second Opium War (1861). The Polish Consulate is still located here today. Walking along its quiet streets feels like stepping back in time. Continue to Yongqingfang on Enning Road, Guangzhou’s longest-preserved arcade street built in 1931. Don’t miss Bruce Lee’s Ancestral Home, a well-preserved Xiguan-style mansion with interactive exhibits, and the Guangdong Opera Art Museum featuring free daytime performances. As dusk falls, view the Canton Tower from the riverside before boarding your Pearl River night cruise for spectacular views of the illuminated skyline.

Day 03
Day 3: June 7
Guangzhou to Zhangjiajie
▾
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Travel from Guangdong’s urban metropolis to Hunan‘s mountain wonderland aboard China’s world-famous high-speed train. The journey offers views of the changing landscape as you travel north into the karst mountains of Zhangjiajie.

After breakfast, transfer to Guangzhou South Railway Station for a high-speed train to Zhangjiajie (approximately 6-7 hours). China‘s high-speed rail network is renowned for its punctuality and comfort. As you travel north, watch the landscape transform from the Pearl River Delta’s dense urban fabric to the lush, karst-studded hills of Hunan province. Upon arrival at Zhangjiajie West Station, your local guide will meet you and escort you to your hotel. Rest and prepare for the breathtaking natural wonders awaiting you.

Day 04
Day 4: June 8
Zhangjiajie | Yuanjiajie & Tianzi Mountain (Group Tour)
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Avatar Hallelujah Mountain — Stand before the 1,074-meter quartz-sandstone pillar that inspired the floating mountains in James Cameron’s Avatar. The Southern Sky Column was officially renamed “Hallelujah Mountain“ in 2010.
Bailong Elevator — Ride the world’s tallest outdoor elevator (326 meters), built directly into a cliff face. The 66-second ascent offers spectacular views as you emerge from the rock.
First Bridge Under Heaven — Walk across a natural stone arch connecting two peaks with a 350-meter sheer drop below.
Enchanting Terrace — Enjoy panoramic views from this classic platform where hundreds of densely distributed peaks create a spectacular scene.
Ten-Mile Gallery — Admire natural rock formations lining a picturesque valley, best viewed from the sightseeing train.

Today you explore the heart of the Wulingyuan Scenic Area, a UNESCO World Heritage site and China‘s first national forest park. Begin with the Bailong Elevator, an engineering marvel that transports visitors from the valley floor to the heights of Yuanjiajie in just 1.5 minutes. At Yuanjiajie, the highlight is Hallelujah Mountain, the pillar that inspired the floating mountains in Avatar. When clouds and mist appear after rain, the bottom is covered, making the pillar appear as if floating in the air. Nearby, walk across the First Bridge Under Heaven, a natural rocky bridge about 2 meters wide spanning 25 meters between two peaks. Step on the bridge and you can overlook the deep abyss and scattered vegetation beneath. Visit Enchanting Terrace, the most classic viewing platform in Yuanjiajie, where you can see hundreds of peaks densely distributed. After lunch, visit Tianzi Mountain, accessible via cable car, offering panoramic views of thousands of stone pillars. Conclude at Ten-Mile Gallery, where a series of peaks line a picturesque valley.

Day 05
Day 5: June 9
Zhangjiajie | Tianmen Mountain & Train to Chongqing
▾
✈ TransportCable car, High-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tianmen Mountain Cable Car — Experience one of the world‘s longest cable car rides (7,455 meters, 28 minutes), ascending 1,279 meters directly from the city center to the summit.
Glass Skywalk — Challenge your courage on the 60-meter glass-bottomed walkway suspended on a sheer cliff face. Shoe covers are required (RMB 5).
Heaven’s Gate (Tianmen Cave) — Witness the world‘s highest natural karst arch, a massive hole 131.5 meters high and 57 meters wide punched through the mountain. Descend the 999 steps—a number symbolizing eternity.
Tujia Folk Customs Park — Explore the cultural heritage of the Tujia ethnic group, the predominant indigenous people of the Zhangjiajie region.

Today you explore Tianmen Mountain, Zhangjiajie‘s iconic city-side peak. Take the cable car from the city center to the summit—one of the world’s longest. During the ascent, you‘ll see the famous 99-bend Tongtian Avenue (Road to Heaven) winding like a silver ribbon below. At the summit, follow the West Route trail. The Glass Skywalk is a 60-meter walkway suspended against a vertical cliff face—an exhilarating test of nerve. Continue along the Guigu Plank Road, hugging the mountain‘s contours. A series of mountain-penetrating escalators carry you down to Tianmen Cave (Heaven‘s Gate), a natural arch formed in 263 AD when an earthquake split the mountain open. From the cave, descend the 999 steps to the base—a number symbolizing eternity in Chinese culture. After descending, visit Tujia Folk Customs Park to learn about Tujia culture, traditional stilted houses, and the ancient Tusi chieftain system. Then transfer to Zhangjiajie West Station for a high-speed train to Chongqing (approximately 5 hours). Upon arrival, your guide will escort you to your hotel.

Day 06
Day 6: June 10
Chongqing | Mountain City Heart & Hongyadong
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Jiefangbei — Visit the symbolic heart of Chongqing, the Liberation Monument, surrounded by towering skyscrapers and bustling commercial streets.
Bayi Food Street — Dive into Chongqing’s legendary food scene on this bustling pedestrian street. Must-try items include hot-and-sour rice noodles (suan la fen), grilled pork brain (kao nao hua), and glutinous rice balls.
Kuixinglou — Discover a restored historical complex offering insight into Chongqing‘s traditional architecture.
Hongyadong Night View — Experience Chongqing’s most iconic sight: a multi-level complex of traditional stilted houses built into a cliffside, illuminated with golden lights after dark.

Begin at Jiefangbei (Liberation Monument), the symbolic center of Chongqing. Originally built to commemorate China‘s victory over Japan in World War II, it now stands at the heart of a dizzying commercial district surrounded by skyscrapers. Walk to Bayi Food Street, Chongqing’s most famous culinary destination. The street is packed with stalls serving the city’s legendary cuisine—especially hot pot. Must-try items include suan la fen (hot-and-sour rice noodles), kao nao hua (grilled pork brain), and Jianglong Claws (spiced chicken feet). Visit Kuixinglou, a restored historical complex with traditional courtyard architecture. As dusk falls, experience the magic of Hongyadong, a remarkable complex of traditional diaojiaolou (stilted houses) built against a steep cliff overlooking the Jialing River. By night, it transforms into a spectacular display of golden lights that cascade down the cliff face—reminiscent of the bathhouse in Spirited Away.

Day 07
Day 7: June 11
Chongqing to Chengdu | Urban Wonders & Train
▾
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Liziba Light Rail Station — Witness one of Chongqing’s most famous urban oddities—a light rail train that passes directly through a residential building.
Eling No.2 Factory — Explore a repurposed industrial site transformed into a creative hub with art galleries, designer studios, and panoramic Yangtze River views.
Ciqikou Ancient Town — Step back in time at this well-preserved riverside town with over 1,000 years of history. Try local specialties like Chen Mahua (fried dough twists) and visit Baolun Temple.

Begin at the Liziba Light Rail Station viewing platform, where you can watch Line 2 pass directly through a residential building—a remarkable feat of engineering that exemplifies Chongqing‘s creative approach to urban planning. Next, visit Eling No.2 Factory, a former industrial complex reimagined as a vibrant arts and culture district with sweeping views of the Yangtze River. Then head to Ciqikou Ancient Town, a historic riverside enclave dating back over a millennium, where narrow alleys, traditional teahouses, and local delicacies like Chen Mahua await. Afterward, transfer to Chongqing West Station for your high-speed train to Chengdu East Station (approx. 1.5–2 hours). Upon arrival, your guide will meet you and escort you to your hotel.

Wuyuan feels like stepping into a hand-painted scroll—centuries-old Huizhou-style villages with white-washed walls, charcoal-grey tile roofs, and intricate wooden lattice windows, many dating back to the Ming and Qing dynasties. You’ll wander narrow stone lanes lined with century-old camphor trees, pause at quiet village ponds where locals still wash vegetables, and cross arched granite bridges built in the 12th century—some still bearing faint inscriptions from their original masons. Spring brings fields of rapeseed flowers glowing gold against the misty hills, while autumn softens the palette to amber and rust, especially around Likeng and Jiangwan, two of the best-preserved villages that remain lived-in, not staged. It’s a place where time moves with the rhythm of tea harvests and river mist—not fast, not slow, just quietly present.
