Shenzhen → Zhangjiajie
▾
🗺Shenzhen → Zhangjiajie
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Travel north from Shenzhen to Zhangjiajie by high-speed train (approx. 4 hours). Nestled in the mountains of Hunan Province, Zhangjiajie is famous for its dramatic quartz sandstone pillars, misty forests, and UNESCO-listed landscapes — the inspiration for Avatar’s Hallelujah Mountains. Upon arrival, transfer to your mountain hotel and spend the afternoon adjusting to the cool, fresh mountain air. You may take a short stroll through the nearby town to soak in the peaceful atmosphere before your adventure begins.

You travel from Shenzhen to Zhangjiajie via high-speed train (approximately 4 hours), entering the mist-shrouded, otherworldly landscape of Hunan Province. Known for its towering sandstone pillars and lush biodiversity, Zhangjiajie is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the real-life inspiration for Pandora in Avatar. After arrival, you check into your mountain hotel and acclimate to the cooler, serene environment — perhaps with a quiet walk through town to absorb the tranquil rhythm of life before the full exploration begins.

Day 04
Day 4 | August 20
Zhangjiajie Sightseeing
▾
🗺Zhangjiajie National Forest Park → Bailong Elevator → Yuanjiajie → Tianzi Mountain
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Zhangjiajie National Forest Park — the crown jewel of the region
Bailong Elevator — the world’s tallest outdoor elevator, rising 326 meters through the cliffside
Yuanjiajie — jagged, mist-shrouded stone pillars creating the iconic 'floating mountains'
Tianzi Mountain — highest point in the park, offering sweeping views of layered peaks and waterfalls
Sunset view over the forest as mountains glow golden

Your full-day exploration of Zhangjiajie National Forest Park begins with the awe-inspiring Bailong Elevator, ascending 326 meters up a sheer cliff face to reveal panoramic vistas of the forest below. You then hike through Yuanjiajie’s surreal landscape of towering sandstone pillars veiled in mist — the famed 'floating mountains' that inspired Avatar. The day culminates at Tianzi Mountain, where sweeping views of cascading waterfalls and layered peaks unfold, followed by a magical sunset bathing the entire forest in golden light.

Day 05
Day 5 | August 21
Zhangjiajie Sightseeing
▾
🗺Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon → Glass Bridge → BaoFeng Lake → 72 Tujia Towers
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon — home to the world-famous Glass Bridge (430 meters long, suspended 300 meters above canyon floor)
BaoFeng Lake — crystal-clear alpine lake surrounded by lush green peaks and waterfalls
72 Tujia Towers — traditional village celebrating Tujia ethnic minority culture, featuring wooden towers, folk performances, hanging houses, and embroidery traditions

Today takes you to the dramatic Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon, where you cross the heart-pounding Glass Bridge — a 430-meter-long structure suspended 300 meters above the canyon floor. A peaceful boat ride on BaoFeng Lake follows, gliding across its mirror-like surface framed by emerald cliffs and waterfalls. In the afternoon, you immerse yourself in Tujia culture at the 72 Tujia Towers, exploring historic wooden architecture, watching vibrant folk performances, and learning about centuries-old customs including hanging houses and intricate embroidery.

Day 06
Day 6 | August 22
Zhangjiajie Sightseeing
▾
🗺Tianmen Mountain → Cable Car → Glass Walkway → Heaven’s Gate
✈ TransportCable car, private car
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Tianmen Mountain — known as the 'Gateway to Heaven'
Cable car ride up the mountain with sweeping views of the winding road below
Glass Walkway — narrow cliffside path with glass floors offering vertiginous valley views
Heaven’s Gate — 131-meter-tall natural archway reached by climbing 999 steps; a sacred site revered for centuries

You ascend Tianmen Mountain — dubbed the 'Gateway to Heaven' — via one of the world’s longest cable car rides, revealing spectacular views of the serpentine 'Nine-Bend Road' far below. At the summit, you brave the thrilling Glass Walkway, a narrow path clinging to the cliff edge with transparent floors plunging into the valley. The spiritual highlight is Heaven’s Gate: a majestic natural arch formed by erosion, approached by the symbolic climb of 999 steps — a centuries-old pilgrimage site steeped in reverence and legend.

Day 7 | August 23
Zhangjiajie → Xi’an

🗺Zhangjiajie → Xi’an
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Travel from Zhangjiajie to Xi’an by high-speed train (approx. 6 hours). Upon arrival, transfer to your hotel in the city center. Spend the evening exploring the nearby streets, or head to the Muslim Quarter to sample local snacks like roujiamo (Chinese hamburger) and biangbiang noodles, while soaking in the lively atmosphere of the night markets.

You board a high-speed train for the ~6-hour journey from Zhangjiajie to Xi’an, transitioning from misty mountain terrain to the ancient heartland of Chinese civilization. Upon arrival in Xi’an, you’re transferred to your centrally located hotel. The evening is yours to wander — perhaps strolling tree-lined avenues or diving straight into the sensory explosion of the Muslim Quarter, where the aroma of sizzling lamb skewers, the crunch of roujiamo, and the bustle of night markets welcome you to 13 dynasties’ worth of history.

Day 08
Day 8 | August 24
Xi’an Sightseeing
▾
🗺Xi’an Ancient City Wall → Hanyang Mausoleum → Xi’an Museum
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Xi’an Ancient City Wall — largest and best-preserved city wall in China (13.7 km)
Hanyang Mausoleum — tomb of Emperor Jing of the Han Dynasty, featuring miniature terracotta figurines and Han artifacts
Xi’an Museum — vast collection of relics from 3,000 years of history, including Tang Dynasty pottery, jade, and bronze

You begin with the Xi’an Ancient City Wall — a monumental 13.7-kilometer fortification encircling the old city — where renting a bicycle lets you cycle atop the ramparts and absorb views blending ancient battlements with modern skylines. Next, you visit the Hanyang Mausoleum to encounter delicate miniature terracotta figures and artifacts illuminating daily life during the Han Dynasty. The day concludes at the Xi’an Museum, housing millennia-spanning treasures — from Shang bronzes to Tang ceramics — offering deep context for the city’s unparalleled historical continuity.

Day 09
Day 9 | August 25
Xi’an Sightseeing
▾
🗺Terracotta Army Museum → Clay Warrior Making Experience → Grand Mosque → Muslim Quarter
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Terracotta Army Museum — UNESCO World Heritage Site with thousands of life-sized, uniquely detailed soldiers
Clay Warrior Making Experience — hands-on workshop to mold and paint your own miniature terracotta soldier
Grand Mosque — stunning fusion of Chinese architectural style and Islamic elements
Muslim Quarter — bustling market with street food stalls, spice shops, and handicraft vendors

The morning immerses you in the awe of the Terracotta Army Museum, where three massive pits house thousands of life-sized warriors — each individually sculpted with distinct faces, armor, and weaponry — built to guard Emperor Qin Shi Huang in eternity. You then craft your own legacy with a Clay Warrior Making Experience, learning traditional techniques to mold and paint a souvenir. In the afternoon, you visit the Grand Mosque — a harmonious blend of Chinese courtyard design and Islamic faith — before losing yourself in the vibrant Muslim Quarter, feasting on lamb skewers, sweet rice cakes, and aromatic spices amid centuries-old alleyways.

Xi’an → Beijing

🗺Xi’an → Beijing
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Travel from Xi’an to Beijing by high-speed train (approx. 4.5 hours). Upon arrival, transfer to your hotel in the city center. Spend the evening at leisure: you may take a walk through the historic hutongs (narrow alleyways), visit a local tea house, or relax at your hotel after the journey.

You board a high-speed train for the ~4.5-hour journey eastward from Xi’an to Beijing, crossing the North China Plain toward the nation’s political and cultural capital. Upon arrival, you’re transferred to your centrally located hotel. The evening is intentionally unhurried — an opportunity to unwind, perhaps meander through atmospheric hutongs lined with courtyard homes, sip fragrant jasmine tea in a traditional teahouse, or simply reflect on the profound historical layers you’ve just traversed.

Day 11
Day 11 | August 27
Beijing Sightseeing
▾
🗺Tiananmen Square → Forbidden City → Temple of Heaven
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsB/L/D
Tiananmen Square — symbolic heart of China
Forbidden City — former imperial palace of Ming and Qing dynasties, home to 24 emperors for over 500 years
Temple of Heaven — masterpiece of Chinese architecture where emperors prayed for good harvests; Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests with blue-tiled roof and wooden carvings

Your Beijing exploration opens at Tiananmen Square — the vast ceremonial plaza anchoring China’s modern identity — before stepping into the Forbidden City, where ornate halls, gilded roofs, and silent courtyards whisper tales of imperial intrigue and power spanning five centuries. The day closes at the Temple of Heaven, whose perfectly symmetrical, azure-roofed Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests stands as a pinnacle of Ming-era cosmology and craftsmanship; you stroll its tranquil park grounds as locals practice tai chi and fly kites beneath ancient cypress trees.
