Day 01
Day 1
Beijing | Imperial Heart
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Tian’anmen Square → Forbidden City (Palace Museum) → Jingshan Park → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tian’anmen Square — Stand in the world’s largest public square, surrounded by the Great Hall of the People, National Museum, and Chairman Mao Memorial Hall.
Forbidden City — Explore the largest and best-preserved ancient palatial complex in the world, home to 24 Ming and Qing emperors over nearly 500 years.
Jingshan Park — Climb the artificial hill behind the Forbidden City for the quintessential panoramic view of the imperial palace‘s golden roofs.

Begin your Beijing exploration at Tian’anmen Square, the symbolic heart of China. Enter the Forbidden City through the imposing Tian‘anmen Gate. Built between 1406 and 1420, this UNESCO World Heritage site served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors. Your tour follows the central axis: across the Golden River Bridges, through the Gate of Supreme Harmony, and into the courtyard of the Hall of Supreme Harmony, where emperors conducted state ceremonies. Continue through the Inner Court, including the Palace of Heavenly Purity and the Imperial Garden. Exit through the north gate and cross to Jingshan Park. Climb Coal Hill to Wanchun Pavilion for the definitive view of the Forbidden City’s golden roofs.

Day 2
Beijing | Imperial Gardens & Modern Olympics
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Summer Palace → Old Summer Palace (Yuanmingyuan) → Olympic Park (Bird‘s Nest & Water Cube exterior) → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Summer Palace — Explore the largest and best-preserved imperial garden in China, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Highlights include the Long Corridor, Marble Boat, and Kunming Lake.
Old Summer Palace (Yuanmingyuan) — Wander through the haunting ruins of the “Garden of Gardens,“ destroyed by Anglo-French forces in 1860, featuring the evocative Western Mansions stone archways.
Olympic Park — View the iconic Bird’s Nest (National Stadium) and Water Cube (National Aquatics Center) from the 2008 Beijing Olympics.

Begin at the Summer Palace, a masterpiece of Chinese landscape architecture. Walk the Long Corridor, a 728-meter covered walkway with over 14,000 traditional paintings, then visit the Marble Boat and enjoy a stroll along Kunming Lake. Next, visit the Old Summer Palace, once a vast complex of gardens and palaces. The ruins of the Western Mansions, with crumbling stone arches and carved columns, are a poignant reminder of China‘s complex history. In the afternoon, drive to Olympic Park to view the iconic Bird’s Nest and Water Cube from the 2008 Beijing Olympics.

Day 3
Beijing to Shanghai | Great Wall & Temple of Heaven
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Mutianyu Great Wall (cable car ascent) → Lunch → Temple of Heaven → Transfer to Beijing Capital Airport → Flight MU5186 to Shanghai (19:00 – 21:10) → Shanghai airport pick-up → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportPrivate car, Domestic flight
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Mutianyu Great Wall — Walk along one of the most beautifully restored sections of the Great Wall, with stunning mountain views. Cable car provides easy access.
Temple of Heaven — Visit the magnificent UNESCO World Heritage site where emperors prayed for good harvests, featuring the iconic Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests.

Drive to Mutianyu Great Wall (approx. 1.5 hours). Take the cable car up to the wall and walk along this well-preserved section with 22 watchtowers. After lunch, visit the Temple of Heaven, built in 1420. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests is a triple-eaved circular structure entirely of wood without nails. Transfer to the airport for your evening flight to Shanghai. Upon arrival, your guide will escort you to your hotel.

Shanghai | The Bund & Modern Skyline
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → The Bund → Lujiazui (Shanghai Tower or Oriental Pearl Tower viewing platform, optional) → Nanjing Road → Evening: The Bund night view → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
The Bund — Stroll along Shanghai’s iconic waterfront, where 26 colonial-era buildings face the futuristic Pudong skyline.
Lujiazui — Cross the Huangpu River to Pudong’s financial district, home to the Shanghai Tower, Shanghai World Financial Center, and Oriental Pearl Tower.
Nanjing Road — Walk along China’s premier shopping street, bustling with energy and neon lights.

Begin at The Bund, Shanghai’s most famous waterfront promenade, featuring buildings in Gothic, Baroque, and Renaissance styles. Cross the Huangpu River via tunnel or ferry to Lujiazui for up-close views of the towering skyscrapers. Return to Nanjing Road for shopping and people-watching. As night falls, return to The Bund to see the illuminated colonial buildings and Pudong skyline reflecting in the river.

Tianzifang — Explore a labyrinthine arts and crafts enclave within traditional shikumen alleyways.
Yuyuan Garden — Visit a classical Ming Dynasty garden (1559) with exquisite pavilions, rockeries, and ponds.
City God Temple — Explore the vibrant Taoist temple complex and bustling bazaar.

Walk along Wukang Road in the former French Concession, seeing the historic Wukang Building. Then explore Tianzifang, a maze of narrow alleys filled with art studios, boutiques, and cafes. After lunch, visit Yuyuan Garden, a classical garden with zigzag bridges and rockeries. Adjacent is the City God Temple and its lively bazaar, perfect for souvenir shopping and sampling local snacks.

Day 7
Xi‘an | Terracotta Army & Tang Dynasty Spectacle
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Terracotta Army (approx. 1 hour) → Terracotta Army Museum (Pit 1, 2, 3) → Lunch → Huaqing Palace → Evening: The Everlasting Regret Show → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Terracotta Army — Stand before thousands of life-sized terracotta warriors, each with unique facial features, guarding the tomb of China‘s first emperor.
Huaqing Palace — Visit the historic hot spring palace associated with Emperor Xuanzong and his beloved concubine Yang Guifei.
The Everlasting Regret Show — Witness a spectacular outdoor performance depicting the tragic love story of Emperor Xuanzong and Yang Guifei, set against the backdrop of Huaqing Palace.

Drive to the Terracotta Army museum. Pit 1 contains over 6,000 warriors in battle formation. Pits 2 and 3 feature cavalry, chariots, and the command post. After lunch, visit Huaqing Palace, a Tang Dynasty imperial retreat with hot springs. In the evening, enjoy The Everlasting Regret Show, a grand outdoor production with elaborate costumes, music, and special effects telling the famous love story.

Xi‘an | City Wall, History Museum & Pagoda
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Xi’an City Wall (optional bike rental) → Shaanxi History Museum → Lunch → Big Wild Goose Pagoda → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Xi’an City Wall — Walk or bike along the most complete and best-preserved ancient city wall in China, dating to the Ming Dynasty (1370).
Shaanxi History Museum — Explore one of China‘s finest museums, housing over 370,000 artifacts from the province’s 3,000-year history.
Big Wild Goose Pagoda — Visit the iconic 64-meter Buddhist pagoda built in 652 AD to house sutras brought from India by the monk Xuanzang.

Ascend the Xi‘an City Wall, stretching 14 kilometers. Rent a bicycle to ride along the top. Next, visit the Shaanxi History Museum, with highlights including Zhou bronzes, Qin terracotta artifacts, and Tang gold and silver wares. After lunch, visit the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. Climb to the top for panoramic views of Xi’an. The surrounding square features a large musical fountain show.

After lunch, transfer to the station for a high-speed train to Lanzhou. Arrive in the evening and check into your hotel.

Zhengning Road Night Market — Experience Lanzhou’s famous night market, offering local specialties like Lanzhou hand-pulled noodles, grilled lamb skewers, and sweet rice wine.

Next, walk across the historic Zhongshan Bridge over the Yellow River. Nearby, see the Yellow River Mother Statue, depicting a mother and child representing the river‘s nurturing role. In the evening, explore Zhengning Road Night Market, a lively food street where you can sample authentic Lanzhou cuisine.

Zhangye to Dunhuang

Giant Buddha Temple — Visit the largest reclining Buddha statue in China, a 34.5-meter-long clay figure dating from the Western Xia Dynasty (1098 AD), housed in a magnificent wooden hall.

Start your day at the Giant Buddha Temple in Zhangye, also known as the Reclining Buddha Temple. The centerpiece is a 34.5-meter-long reclining Buddha statue, one of the largest of its kind in the world. Mogao Caves — Explore this UNESCO World Heritage site, a treasure trove of Buddhist art with over 700 caves dating from the 4th to 14th centuries, featuring exquisite murals, sculptures, and the famous Library Cave.
Mingsha Mountain & Crescent Lake — Experience the desert landscape with singing sand dunes and a crescent-shaped spring that has never dried up. Take a camel ride or climb the dunes for spectacular sunset views.

Visit the Mogao Caves, also known as the Thousand Buddha Grottoes. Highlights include Cave 16/17 (the Library Cave) and the massive seated Buddha. In the afternoon, head to Mingsha Mountain (Echoing Sand Mountain) and Crescent Lake. You can ride a camel across the dunes, walk up the sand slope, or simply enjoy the view of this natural oasis. Sunset over the dunes is spectacular.

Day 14
Dunhuang

🗺Hotel breakfast → Western Line day tour: Yumen Pass → Yangguan Pass → Lunch → Yadan Devil City (Yadan National Geopark) → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotelhotel in Dunhuang
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Yumen Pass — Visit the famous Jade Gate Pass, a strategic military outpost on the ancient Silk Road. The name comes from jade transported from Central Asia through this pass.
Yangguan Pass — Explore the Southern Jade Gate, another key Silk Road fortification. The area features ruins of watchtowers and beacon towers.
Yadan Devil City — Discover a surreal landscape of wind-eroded rock formations (yardangs) in the Gobi Desert. The unique shapes and howling winds create an eerie atmosphere.

Embark on a full-day western route tour of Dunhuang’s historic frontier passes. First, visit Yumen Pass, where you’ll see the ruins of a Han Dynasty watchtower and the surrounding Gobi landscape. Next, drive to Yangguan Pass, the southern counterpart. After lunch, continue to the Yadan National Geopark (Yadan Devil City), a vast area of wind-carved rock formations. Shuttle buses take you through the park to view formations resembling ships, castles, and animals. Sunset in this landscape is particularly dramatic. Return to Dunhuang in the evening.

Qinghai Lake — Visit China’s largest inland saltwater lake, situated at 3,200 meters above sea level. The lake‘s deep blue waters contrast with surrounding grasslands and distant snow-capped mountains.
Chaka Salt Lake — Experience the “Mirror of the Sky“—a vast salt flat where the shallow water reflects the sky, creating stunning photo opportunities (weather permitting). You can walk on the salt crust and take a small train across the lake.

Drive to Qinghai Lake, a sacred site for Tibetan Buddhists and a haven for migratory birds. Stop at scenic viewpoints along the southern shore. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Afternoon, drive to Chaka Salt Lake, located in the Qaidam Basin. The lake‘s high salt concentration creates a mirror-like surface, perfect for photos. You can take a small train ride out onto the lake and wade in the shallow water (bring sandals). Return to Xining in the evening.
