Welcome to Guangzhou, the vibrant capital of Guangdong province and one of China’s most dynamic cities. After your international flight, your guide will meet you at the airport and escort you to your hotel. Today is dedicated to rest and recovery.

Your China journey begins in Guangzhou, a city that has served as a major trading port for centuries. Known historically as Canton, it remains one of China’s most cosmopolitan destinations. Day 2
Guangzhou | Heritage & Colonial Charm
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Chen Clan Academy → Yuexiu Park → Shamian Island → Yongqingfang → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Chen Clan Academy — Explore a stunning example of traditional Lingnan architecture, featuring intricate wood carvings, stone sculptures, and ceramic decorations.
Yuexiu Park — Visit Guangzhou’s largest park, home to the iconic Five Rams Statue—the symbol of the city—and the ancient Zhenhai Tower.
Shamian Island — Stroll through a peaceful colonial-era enclave where European consulates and mansions from the 19th century stand amidst tree-lined boulevards.
Yongqingfang — Wander through a beautifully restored historical neighborhood where traditional Cantonese architecture meets modern art galleries and boutique shops.

Begin at Chen Clan Academy, a magnificent complex representing the pinnacle of traditional Lingnan artistry. Built between 1890 and 1894, it served as a study hall for Chen family members preparing for imperial examinations. The intricate carvings and ceramics throughout showcase the craftsmanship of the late Qing Dynasty.

Next, visit Yuexiu Park, a sprawling green oasis in the heart of the city. The park’s centerpiece is the Five Rams Statue, commemorating the legend of five immortals who rode into Guangzhou on rams carrying rice—a story that gave the city its nickname “City of Rams.” Climb Zhenhai Tower, a five-story structure built in 1380, which now houses the Guangzhou Museum.

After lunch, explore Shamian Island, a sandbar island that became a foreign enclave following the Opium Wars. Walking along its quiet streets feels like stepping back in time—colonial-era buildings and banyan trees draped with aerial roots make this a favorite spot for photographers.

Continue to Yongqingfang, a revitalized historical district nestled along the Liwan Lake. This area showcases traditional Cantonese “qilou” (arcade-style) buildings, blending ancient charm with contemporary creativity—traditional herb shops sit alongside stylish cafes and art galleries.

Take a Pearl River Cruise for a different perspective of the city. The cruise offers unobstructed views of the Canton Tower, the modern architecture of Zhujiang New Town, and the historic buildings along the riverbanks.

In the afternoon, explore Beijing Road, the city’s premier shopping destination for centuries. Look down—glass-covered archaeological pits along the street showcase ancient road surfaces from the Tang, Song, and Ming dynasties. Continue to Shangxiajiu Road, another historic shopping thoroughfare dominated by classic qilou architecture. Here, you’ll find a more local shopping experience: dried seafood shops, traditional Chinese medicine stores, and family-run restaurants.

Chongqing | Modern Marvels & Culinary Delights
▾

✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Liziba Light Rail Station — Witness one of Chongqing’s most famous urban oddities—a light rail train that passes directly through a residential building.
Three Gorges Museum — Explore this comprehensive museum dedicated to the history, culture, and construction of the Three Gorges Dam, with fascinating exhibits on the Yangtze River region.
Bayi Food Street — Dive into Chongqing’s legendary food scene on this bustling pedestrian street, sampling authentic hot pot, spicy noodles, and local snacks.

Begin at the Liziba Light Rail Station viewing platform, where you can watch Line 2 of the city’s light rail system pass directly through a residential building—not above it or beside it, but through the middle—a remarkable feat of engineering.

Next, visit the Three Gorges Museum, one of Chongqing’s premier cultural institutions. The museum offers comprehensive exhibits on the history, culture, and archaeology of the Yangtze River region, with a focus on the Three Gorges area. Highlights include ancient bronze artifacts, Han Dynasty stone carvings, and exhibits on the construction of the Three Gorges Dam.

In the evening, visit Bayi Food Street, Chongqing’s most famous culinary destination. The street is packed with restaurants and food stalls serving the city’s legendary cuisine—particularly hot pot. The city is the birthplace of hot pot, and Bayi Street offers everything from traditional spicy hot pot to street snacks like spicy rabbit head, dan dan noodles, and chuan chuan (skewers). For a grand finale, consider a night cruise on the Yangtze and Jialing rivers to view Chongqing’s spectacular illuminated skyline from the water.

Zhangjiajie | Tianmen Mountain
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Tianmen Mountain Scenic Area → Cable car ascent → Glass Skywalk → Guigu Plank Road → Mountain-penetrating escalator → Tianmen Cave (Heaven’s Gate) → 999 steps descent → Cable car down → Return to hotel
✈ TransportCable car, private car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tianmen Mountain Cable Car — Experience one of the world’s longest cable car rides (30 minutes), ascending 1,279 meters directly from the city center to the mountain summit.
Glass Skywalk — Challenge your courage on the 60-meter glass-bottomed walkway suspended on a sheer cliff face.
Heaven’s Gate — Witness the world’s highest natural karst arch, a massive hole 131.5 meters high, and descend the 999 steps.

Today you explore Tianmen Mountain, Zhangjiajie’s iconic city-side peak. The adventure begins with the Tianmen Mountain Cable Car—one of the world’s longest, rising directly from the city center to the summit. During the 30-minute journey, watch the urban landscape shrink below, with the famous 99-bend Tongtian Avenue winding like a silver ribbon far below.

At the summit, follow the West Line trail. The Glass Skywalk is a 60-meter walkway suspended against a vertical cliff face with a transparent floor revealing the valley far below. Continue along the Guigu Plank Road, hugging the mountain’s contours.

A series of mountain-penetrating escalators carry you down through the mountain’s interior. You’ll emerge at Tianmen Cave (Heaven’s Gate), a natural arch 131.5 meters high. From the cave, descend the 999 steps to the base—a number symbolizing eternity in Chinese culture.

Conclude at Ten-Mile Gallery, where a series of peaks line a picturesque valley. Take the sightseeing train (optional) to view formations like “Old Man Collecting Herbs” and “Sisters Peak.”
