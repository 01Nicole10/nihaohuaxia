Day 02
Day 2: June 8
Shanghai to Suzhou | Classical Gardens & Historic Streets
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Suzhou (approx. 1.5 hours) → Lion Grove Garden → Humble Administrator‘s Garden → Lunch → Pingjiang Road → Guanqian Street → Shantang Street → Check into Suzhou hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelSuzhou hotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Lion Grove Garden — Explore this classical garden famous for its intricate rockery maze, considered the finest example of lion-shaped rock formations in China. Built in 1342, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
Humble Administrator‘s Garden — Visit Suzhou’s largest and most renowned classical garden, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in 1513, it features exquisite pavilions, lotus ponds, and rockeries.
Pingjiang Road — Stroll along an 800-year-old canal-side street, where traditional shops, teahouses, and snack stalls preserve the atmosphere of old Suzhou.
Shantang Street — Walk along this historic canal street stretching from the Changmen Gate to Tiger Hill, often described as “a scene of paradise on earth.”

After breakfast, drive to Suzhou, a city celebrated for its classical gardens, silk production, and intricate canal networks. Begin at Lion Grove Garden, the only UNESCO garden in Suzhou renowned for its rockery maze. Built in 1342 by a Buddhist monk, the garden features artificial mountains made of limestone resembling lions in various postures—roaring, sleeping, or playing. The labyrinth of caves and peaks invites exploration.

Next, visit the Humble Administrator‘s Garden, a masterpiece of classical Chinese garden design spanning over five hectares. Built in 1513, the garden is divided into eastern, central, and western sections. The central section features the expansive Lotus Pond with its pavilions and bridges, while the western section offers intimate spaces with rockeries and waterfalls. The garden exemplifies the principles of classical Chinese landscape design: every rock, pond, and pavilion is arranged to create a harmonious microcosm of nature.

After lunch, walk along Pingjiang Road, one of Suzhou’s best-preserved historic streets. The canal-side lane has remained largely unchanged for eight centuries. Here, you can sample local snacks like sweet osmanthus cakes, visit traditional silk embroidery workshops, or simply watch the boats glide by from a teahouse balcony.

Continue to Guanqian Street, Suzhou’s main commercial pedestrian street, bustling with shops and local eateries. Then explore Shantang Street, a historic canal street stretching about 3.6 kilometers from the Changmen Gate to Tiger Hill. Known as the “first street of Suzhou,” it was a favorite of Emperor Qianlong during his southern tours. The street is lined with traditional architecture, stone bridges, and willow trees, offering a picturesque scene.

Day 3: June 9
Suzhou to Wuxi | Lingshan Grand Buddha
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Drive to Wuxi (approx. 1 hour) → Lingshan Grand Buddha Scenic Area → Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance → Brahma Palace → Check into Wuxi hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 HotelWuxi hotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Lingshan Grand Buddha — Stand before the 88-meter bronze Buddha statue, one of the largest in the world. Climb the 218 steps to reach the base for a panoramic view of Taihu Lake.
Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance — Witness the spectacular fountain show depicting the birth of Prince Siddhartha, where nine dragon statues spray water as the golden statue of the infant Buddha rotates.
Brahma Palace — Explore the “Oriental Louvre,” a magnificent modern structure blending Buddhist art, architecture, and culture.

Drive to Wuxi (approximately 1 hour) to visit the Lingshan Grand Buddha Scenic Area, a sprawling Buddhist cultural theme park at the foot of Longshan Mountain.

Enter through the Five-Ming Bridge and walk along the Bodhi Avenue, lined with prayer flags and lotus-shaped lanterns. The first major attraction is the Nine-Dragon Water Curtain Performance, a spectacular fountain show that runs several times daily. When the performance begins, nine bronze dragon heads spray water toward a 7-meter-tall golden statue of the infant Buddha, which rotates to reveal all sides—a dramatic representation of the Buddhist legend.

Continue to the Brahma Palace, an architectural marvel completed in 2008. The palace‘s interior is breathtaking: marble floors, hand-carved wooden ceilings, and a massive central dome decorated with Buddhist murals. The palace also houses the Great Enlightenment Hall, where the “Splendid Lingshan” performance combines dance, music, and projection mapping.

Finally, approach the 88-meter Grand Buddha. A 218-step staircase leads to the statue’s base. Here, you can “embrace the Buddha‘s feet”—a traditional gesture for seeking blessings. The viewing platform offers sweeping views of Taihu Lake, the third-largest freshwater lake in China.

Day 4: June 10
Wuxi to Shangrao | Wangxian Valley Fairyland
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Transfer to Wuxi Railway Station → High-speed train to Shangrao Station (approx. 4-5 hours) → Private transfer to Wangxian Valley (approx. 1 hour) → Hotel check-in within the scenic area → Evening exploration of illuminated valley
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🏨 HotelHotel within the scenic area
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Journey to Jiangxi province to discover Wangxian Valley, a stunning scenic area built on cliffs and described as a “fairyland town.” As evening falls, the entire valley comes alive with spectacular lighting that transforms the waterfalls, cliffside buildings, and ancient bridges into a magical scene.

After breakfast, transfer to Wuxi Railway Station for a high-speed train to Shangrao, Jiangxi. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours, offering views of the changing landscape as you travel from the flat Jiangnan plains to the mountainous terrain of northeastern Jiangxi.

Upon arrival at Shangrao Station, your local guide will transfer you to Wangxian Valley (about 1 hour). The valley is nestled deep in the mountains of Guangxin District and has gained fame for its dramatic cliffside architecture—traditional houses seemingly clinging to vertical rock faces, connected by winding stone paths and suspended bridges.

Check into your hotel within the scenic area (recommended for the full experience). In the evening, explore the valley when it is most magical. The cliffside buildings are illuminated with warm amber lights, the waterfalls are lit from below, and the suspended bridges glow against the dark mountain backdrop. The reflection of these lights in the valley‘s streams creates a scene reminiscent of a Chinese fantasy painting.

Wangxian Valley to Zhangjiajie | Morning in Fairyland
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Morning exploration of Wangxian Valley: Qingyun Bridge, Glass Plank Walkway, Baizhu Cliff → Lunch → Transfer to Shangrao Station → High-speed train to Zhangjiajie West Station (approx. 6 hours) → Station pick-up → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🏨 HotelHotel

Qingyun Bridge & Glass Plank Walkway — Walk along a gently arching bridge suspended over the canyon, followed by a thrilling glass-bottomed walkway that juts out from the cliff face.
Baizhu Cliff — Ascend to this dramatic viewpoint overlooking the entire valley, where waterfalls plunge into emerald pools.
Scenic Train Journey — Travel from Jiangxi to Hunan on China‘s high-speed rail network, watching the landscape transform into the karst pillars that make Zhangjiajie famous.

Spend your morning exploring the wonders of Wangxian Valley before your journey to Zhangjiajie.

Start at Qingyun Bridge, a picturesque arched stone bridge spanning a deep gorge. Cross to reach the Glass Plank Walkway, a transparent pathway that extends over the canyon—testing your courage while offering spectacular views of the waterfall below. The walkway is designed to blend with the natural rock, creating the illusion of walking on air.

Continue to Baizhu Cliff, one of the valley’s highest viewpoints. From here, you can see the full layout of the cliffside village: wooden houses stacked vertically, connected by stone staircases, with waterfalls cascading through their center. The view is particularly photogenic in the morning light.

After lunch, transfer to Shangrao Station for a high-speed train to Zhangjiajie West Station. The journey takes approximately 6 hours, crossing the provinces of Jiangxi and Hunan. As you travel westward, the terrain becomes increasingly mountainous, culminating in the dramatic quartz-sandstone pillar landscapes that await you.

Upon arrival in Zhangjiajie, your local guide will greet you at the station and escort you to your hotel.

Day 6: June 12
Zhangjiajie | Yuanjiajie, Tianzi Mountain & Bailong Elevator
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Wulingyuan Scenic Area → Shuttle bus → Bailong Elevator ascent → Yuanjiajie exploration (Avatar Hallelujah Mountain, First Bridge Under Heaven) → Shuttle bus → Tianzi Mountain Cable Car → Tianzi Mountain exploration → Return to hotel
✈ TransportShuttle bus, Cable car
🏨 HotelHotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Avatar Hallelujah Mountain — Stand before the 1,074-meter pillar that inspired the floating mountains in James Cameron’s Avatar. The Southern Sky Column was officially renamed “Hallelujah Mountain” in 2010.
Bailong Elevator — Ride the world’s tallest outdoor elevator (326 meters), built directly into a cliff face. The 66-second ascent offers spectacular views as you emerge from the rock.
Tianzi Mountain — Known as the “King of Peak Forests,” this area offers panoramic views of thousands of stone pillars rising from the mist.

Today explores the heart of the Wulingyuan Scenic Area, a UNESCO World Heritage site and China’s first national forest park.

Begin with the Bailong Elevator, an engineering marvel that transports visitors from the valley floor to the heights of Yuanjiajie in just 66 seconds. As you ascend, watch the cliff face slide past and the view expand into a sea of pillars.

At Yuanjiajie, the highlight is Hallelujah Mountain (Southern Sky Column), a 1,074-meter pillar that served as the primary visual reference for the film’s floating Hallelujah Mountains. Nearby, walk across the First Bridge Under Heaven, a natural stone arch connecting two peaks with a sheer drop of 350 meters beneath your feet. The Fascination Terrace offers multiple viewing platforms where pillars seem to float in a sea of green.

After lunch, visit Tianzi Mountain, accessible via cable car. The area’s name means “Son of Heaven Mountain,” commemorating Xiang Dakun, a local farmer who led a peasant uprising in the 14th century and declared himself “Son of Heaven.” From viewing platforms like He Long Park and West Sea, gaze upon a seemingly endless array of stone pillars shrouded in mist.

Zhangjiajie to Furong

🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Tianmen Mountain Scenic Area → Cable car ascent → Glass Skywalk → Guigu Plank Road → Mountain-penetrating escalator → Tianmen Cave (Heaven’s Gate) → 999 steps descent → Cable car down → Drive to Furong Ancient Town (approx. 1.5 hours) → Check into hotel → Evening exploration of Furong
✈ TransportPrivate car, Cable car
🏨 Hotelhotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tianmen Mountain Cable Car — Experience one of the world’s longest cable car rides (30 minutes), ascending 1,279 meters directly from the city center to the mountain summit.
Glass Skywalk — Challenge your courage on the 60-meter glass-bottomed walkway suspended on a sheer cliff face.
Heaven‘s Gate (Tianmen Cave) — Witness the world’s highest natural karst arch, a massive hole 131.5 meters high and 57 meters wide punched through the mountain. Descend the 999 steps—a number symbolizing eternity.
Furong Ancient Town — Discover a 2,000-year-old town perched beside a spectacular waterfall, known as “the town hanging on the waterfall.”

Today you explore Tianmen Mountain, Zhangjiajie’s iconic city-side peak. The adventure begins with the Tianmen Mountain Cable Car—one of the world’s longest, rising directly from the city center to the summit. During the 30-minute journey, you’ll watch the urban landscape shrink below as you ascend into the clouds, with the famous 99-bend Tongtian Avenue (Road to Heaven) winding like a silver ribbon far below.

At the summit, follow the West Line trail. The Glass Skywalk is a 60-meter walkway suspended against a vertical cliff face with a transparent floor revealing the valley far below. Continue along the Guigu Plank Road, hugging the mountain’s contours.

A series of mountain-penetrating escalators carry you down through the mountain‘s interior. You’ll emerge at Tianmen Cave (Heaven‘s Gate), a natural arch 131.5 meters high and 57 meters wide. From the cave, descend the 999 steps to the base—a number symbolizing eternity in Chinese culture.

After descending, drive to Furong Ancient Town (approximately 1.5 hours). Upon arrival, check into your hotel. As evening falls, explore the town’s most distinctive feature: a magnificent waterfall that plunges directly through the settlement—giving it the poetic nickname “the town hanging on the waterfall.“ The Wuli Stone Street, paved with nearly 2,000 five-colored flagstones and lined with traditional Tujia stilted houses, is especially atmospheric after dark.

Day 9: June 15
Shanghai
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Wukang Road → Former French Concession → Nanjing Road → Lunch → Tianzifang → The Bund → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotelhotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Wukang Road — Stroll through the leafy streets of the former French Concession, with plane trees and Art Deco villas. The iconic Wukang Building, a 1924 apartment block designed by Hungarian architect László Hudec, is a must-see landmark.
Nanjing Road — Walk along China‘s premier shopping street, bustling with energy, neon lights, and historic department stores.
Tianzifang — Explore a labyrinthine arts and crafts enclave built within traditional shikumen (stone-gate) alleyways, offering a gritty yet charming mix of art studios, boutiques, and cafes.
The Bund — Stroll along Shanghai’s iconic waterfront, where colonial-era buildings face the futuristic Pudong skyline across the Huangpu River.

Begin your Shanghai exploration in the former French Concession with a walk along Wukang Road. The French Concession is Shanghai at its most atmospheric: plane trees shade quiet lanes lined with 1920s-30s villas and apartment buildings. The star attraction is the Wukang Building, a red-brick, ship-shaped apartment building designed by László Hudec in 1924.

Next, head to Nanjing Road, Shanghai’s premier shopping thoroughfare. This pedestrianized street has been the heart of Shanghai commerce for over a century, lined with flagship stores, historic department stores, and countless shops. The energy here is infectious—street performers, neon signs, and crowds create a vibrant urban spectacle.

After lunch, venture to Tianzifang, hidden within the former French Concession‘s shikumen alleyways. Unlike the polished Xintiandi, Tianzifang retains a more organic, bohemian character. Narrow lanes branch into smaller alleys, revealing unexpected treasures: a ceramics studio here, a craft beer bar there, a traditional paper-cutting artist working in a courtyard.

As dusk falls, make your way to The Bund (Waitan), Shanghai’s most famous waterfront promenade. The 1.5-kilometer stretch features 26 buildings in various architectural styles—Gothic, Baroque, Renaissance—that once housed banks and trading houses during Shanghai’s golden age. Across the Huangpu River, the Pudong New Area presents a futuristic skyline dominated by the Oriental Pearl Tower, Shanghai Tower, and Shanghai World Financial Center.

Day 10: June 16
Shanghai
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Zhujiajiao Ancient Town (approx. 1 hour) → Explore Zhujiajiao: Fangsheng Bridge, Kezhi Garden, canal boat ride → Lunch → Return to Shanghai → Free time → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotelhotel
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Zhujiajiao Ancient Town — Escape to a 1,700-year-old water town on the outskirts of Shanghai, often called the “Venice of Shanghai.“ Explore its ancient stone bridges, traditional houses, and winding canals.
Fangsheng Bridge — Cross the largest and most famous stone arch bridge in Zhujiajiao, dating back to 1571, offering panoramic views of the town’s canals and rooftops.
Kezhi Garden — Visit a classical Jiangnan-style garden built in 1912, featuring exquisite pavilions, rockeries, and a tranquil lotus pond.
Canal Boat Ride — Glide through the narrow waterways in a traditional gondola-style boat for the best perspective on the town’s ancient architecture.

Today you escape the city to visit Zhujiajiao Ancient Town, one of Shanghai’s best-preserved water towns with a history spanning over 1,700 years. Located on the banks of Dianshan Lake, Zhujiajiao is often called the “Venice of Shanghai“ for its intricate network of canals and ancient bridges.

Begin at Fangsheng Bridge, the largest and most famous stone arch bridge in the town. Built in 1571 during the Ming Dynasty, the bridge features elegant arches and offers panoramic views of the canals, traditional houses, and willow trees lining the waterways.

Visit Kezhi Garden, a classical Jiangnan-style garden built in 1912. The garden combines elements of traditional Chinese garden design with Western architectural influences, featuring exquisite pavilions, rockeries, a lotus pond, and winding corridors.

Take a canal boat ride through the narrow waterways, passing beneath ancient stone bridges and alongside whitewashed houses with black-tiled roofs. From the water, you’ll see local residents going about their daily lives—washing clothes on stone steps, hanging laundry from wooden balconies, and tending small gardens.

Explore the town‘s ancient streets, lined with traditional shops selling local specialties: zongzi (sticky rice dumplings), zhajiangmian (noodles with soybean paste), and various snacks. The North Street is particularly famous for its preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.

After lunch, return to Shanghai. The remainder of the day is free for relaxation or last-minute exploration.
