A warm welcome to China! After your long journey, today is all about rest and recovery. Your guide will meet you at the airport and escort you to your hotel, where you can unwind and prepare for the adventure ahead.

Welcome to Zhangjiajie! As you leave the big cities behind and make your way into this prefecture city of Hunan province, you'll notice mountains and hills replacing clusters of skyscrapers. Day 02
Day 2
（group tour）Tian zi Mountain & Yuan jiajie (Avatar Mountains)
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Wulingyuan Scenic Area (ticket ¥228 included) → Shuttle bus (20 min) → Tianzi Mountain Cable Car (¥72 included) → Tianzi Mountain sights (West Sea, He Long Park, Imperial Writing Brush Peak, Fairy Presenting Flowers) → Shuttle bus (40 min) → Yuanjiajie → Lunch at Avatar Restaurant → Afternoon exploration of Yuanjiajie (First Bridge Under Heaven, Avatar Hallelujah Mountain, Heart Lock Bridge, Back Garden, Fascination Terrace) → Bailong Elevator (¥65 included) → Shuttle bus to Ten-Mile Gallery → Optional sightseeing train (¥55 round trip, not included) → Shuttle bus back to Wulingyuan → Dinner → Hotel rest
✈ TransportPrivate car, Shuttle bus, Cable car, Bailong Elevator
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Avatar Hallelujah Mountain: Stand before the real-life inspiration for the floating mountains in James Cameron's Avatar. The Southern Sky Column was officially renamed "Hallelujah Mountain" in 2010 after the film's global success
Bailong Elevator: Ride the world's tallest outdoor elevator (326 meters) built into the side of a cliff. In just 66 seconds, you'll ascend through the mountain for breathtaking views
Tianzi Mountain: Known as the "King of Peak Forests," this area offers panoramic views of thousands of quartz-sandstone pillars — natural wonders formed over millions of years by physical erosion

After breakfast, we enter Wulingyuan Scenic Area, a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site since 1992 and China's first national forest park established in 1982. A short shuttle ride brings us to Tianzi Mountain, where we ascend via cable car. Here, peaks pierce through swirling mists—it's easy to see why Chinese artists have long tried to capture this wonder in paintings. Lunch awaits at the aptly named Avatar Restaurant in Yuanjiajie. This afternoon, we explore the area that made Zhangjiajie internationally famous. The First Bridge Under Heaven is a natural stone arch connecting two peaks, while the Avatar Hallelujah Mountain (formerly Southern Sky Column) juts out from the green abyss like something from another world. We descend via the thrilling Bailong Elevator—watching the mountains fall away beneath you is an unforgettable experience. Finally, we visit Ten-Mile Gallery, where peaks line the valley like a natural art exhibition. You can take the sightseeing train (optional) to comfortably view formations named for their shapes: "Old Man Collecting Herbs," "Sisters Peak," and more.

Day 03
Day 3
（group tour）Tianmen Mountain & Flight to Kunming
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Tianmen Mountain Scenic Area (ticket ¥270 included) → Cable car (30 min, included) → West Line exploration (Glass Skywalk, shoe cover ¥5 included; Guigu Plank Road; Tianmen Mountain Temple) → Summit cable car (¥25 included) → Yunmeng Pavilion (panoramic views) → East Line cable car → Tong Garden → Mountain-penetrating escalator (included) → Tianmen Cave → Descend 999 steps → Cable car down → Shuttle bus to city cable car station → Transfer to Zhangjiajie airport → Flight to Kunming → Kunming airport pick-up → Hotel transfer
✈ TransportPrivate car, Cable car, Domestic flight
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tianmen Mountain Cable Car: Board one of the world's longest cable cars, rising straight from the city to the mountain peak with sweeping views along the way
Glass Skywalk: Test your courage on the "Oriental Sky Road" — a glass walkway suspended on a sheer cliff face. On sunny days, the reflection of blue sky and clouds covers the entire plank road, creating an ethereal floating sensation
Heaven's Gate (Tianmen Cave): Witness the world's highest natural karst arch, a hole punched through the mountain. To reach it, you'll descend 999 steps — a number symbolizing eternity in Chinese culture

Today we explore Tianmen Mountain, Zhangjiajie's most iconic city-side attraction. Rising 1,518.6 meters above sea level, it's the highest mountain in Zhangjiajie's urban area and a cultural holy land known as "the First Holy Mountain in Western Hunan". The cable car ride alone is unforgettable—you'll soar directly from the city to the summit, watching houses shrink to specks below. On the mountain, we follow the West Line, challenging the Glass Skywalk where transparent panels reveal the abyss beneath your feet. The Guigu Plank Road hugs the cliff face, named after the legendary Guigu Cave—a place shrouded in mystery where local warriors have rappelled down for exploration. After visiting Tianmen Mountain Temple, we take a summit cable car to Yunmeng Pavilion for panoramic views. Then, a series of mountain-penetrating escalators (a marvel of engineering!) carry us down through the mountain, emerging at Tianmen Cave—Heaven's Gate itself. The 999 steps down from the cave are a pilgrimage of sorts. Look back up to appreciate the sheer scale of this natural arch. A final cable car and shuttle bus return us to the city, where we transfer to the airport for our flight to Kunming, the "Spring City" of Yunnan province.

Day 4
Kunming to Dali : Erhai Lake & Ancient Town
▾
🗺Kunming hotel breakfast → High-speed train to Dali → Arrival pick-up → Erhai Lake scenic drive → Dali Ancient Town exploration → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportHigh-speed train, Private car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Erhai Lake Scenic Drive: Circle this stunning alpine lake, whose name means "Ear-shaped Sea." The lake stretches 120 kilometers around, offering endless photo opportunities with its crystal-clear waters reflecting the Cangshan Mountains
Dali Ancient Town: Walk through stone streets lined with white-walled houses and traditional Bai ethnic architecture. This ancient town, nestled between the Cangshan Mountains and Erhai Lake, offers a journey back in time

A morning high-speed train brings us to Dali, a place of poetic charm where nature, culture, and tranquility meet. Dali sits between the majestic Cangshan Mountains and the shimmering Erhai Lake, at an elevation of about 1,970 meters, enjoying mild weather year-round. Our Erhai Lake scenic drive takes us along the 120-kilometer lake loop. You'll see why cycling around Erhai is a popular way to enjoy the scenery. We'll stop at picturesque spots for photos—the lake's clarity and the mountain backdrop create postcard-perfect scenes. In the late afternoon, we explore Dali Ancient Town. Cobblestone alleys wind between whitewashed houses with grey-tiled roofs, traditional of Bai architecture. The town pulses with a laid-back, artistic vibe—street musicians play, cafes invite lingering, and artisans sell their crafts.

Day 5
Dali to Lijiang : Ancient Towns & Naxi Culture
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Lijiang (approx. 2.5-3 hours) → Arrival in Lijiang → Lijiang Ancient Town exploration → Baisha Ancient Village visit → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Lijiang Ancient Town: Wander through one of China's four best-preserved ancient cities, a UNESCO World Heritage site where Naxi culture comes alive through architecture, music, and daily life
Baisha Ancient Village: Step back in time at the original capital of the Naxi kingdom. This quiet village offers authentic local life and houses the famous Baisha Frescoes, blending Tibetan Buddhism, Han Buddhism, and Taoist influences

After breakfast, we drive through Yunnan's beautiful countryside to Lijiang, located between the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau and Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. Lijiang is renowned as one of the four best-preserved ancient cities in China, alongside Langzhong, Pingyao, and Shexian. Lijiang Ancient Town (Dayan Old Town) charms visitors with its network of canals, stone bridges, and traditional Naxi architecture. Water runs through the town from the Black Dragon Pool, creating a soundtrack of flowing streams that accompanies your exploration. Small bridges cross canals lined with willow trees—the classic "small bridge, flowing water, people's homes" scene celebrated in Chinese poetry. Later, we visit Baisha Ancient Village, the political, economic, and cultural center of the Naxi people for centuries. Unlike bustling Dayan, Baisha retains a peaceful, authentic atmosphere. Here you can see the famous Baisha Frescoes, exquisite paintings created during the Ming Dynasty (14th-17th centuries) that showcase the fusion of multiple religious traditions.

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain: Ascend via cable car to touch the glaciers of this sacred Naxi mountain. From the valley floor to the peaks, you'll witness a complete vertical spectrum of landscapes — from subtropical to boreal
Blue Moon Valley: Discover turquoise lakes formed by glacial meltwater. On sunny days, the water glows an incredible blue, and the crescent-shaped valley resembles a blue moon embedded at the mountain's foot

Today we visit Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, an extremely high mountain in Yunnan's subtropical zone. From the valley to the peaks, it features a complete vertical band of landscapes—subtropical, temperate, and boreal. The mountain is sacred to the Naxi people, who believe their protective spirits dwell here. We take the large cable car (冰川公园大索道) up to Glacier Park, where you can stand near the eternal snows and glaciers at over 4,500 meters elevation. The views are staggering—peaks stretch in every direction, and on clear days, the sky seems impossibly blue. Descending, we visit Blue Moon Valley (蓝月谷). Day 7
Tiger Leaping Gorge & Journey to Shangri-La
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Drive to Tiger Leaping Gorge → Gorge exploration (elevator included) → Continue to Shangri-La → Dukezong Ancient Town visit → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tiger Leaping Gorge: Stand beside one of the world's deepest and most dramatic gorges, where the Jinsha River (upper Yangtze) rages through a narrow canyon. Legend says a tiger escaped hunters by leaping across at its narrowest point.
Dukezong Ancient Town: Walk through the best-preserved Tibetan town in Yunnan, home to the world's largest prayer wheel. Join locals in turning it for good fortune.

Today we journey toward Shangri-La, following the Jinsha River through increasingly dramatic scenery. Our first stop is Tiger Leaping Gorge, one of the world's deepest gorges, located in the 'Three Parallel Rivers' area—a UNESCO World Heritage site. The gorge gets its name from a legendary tiger that escaped hunters by leaping across at its narrowest point. Standing on the viewing platform, you'll understand why this story persists—the canyon narrows dramatically here, and the river below thunders with incredible force. An elevator (included) helps us access the best viewpoints with ease. Continuing our drive, we ascend toward Shangri-La (Zhongdian), a name borrowed from James Hilton's 1933 novel Lost Horizon.

Day 08
Day 8
Shangri-La: Napa Lake, Songzan lin Monastery & Pudacuo Park
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Napa Lake (Yila Prairie) → Songzanlin Monastery → Pudacuo National Park exploration → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Napa Lake (Yila Prairie): Experience the vast grasslands and wetlands of the Tibetan plateau, where yaks and horses graze against a backdrop of distant snow mountains.
Songzanlin Monastery: Visit the 'Little Potala Palace,' the largest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Yunnan, with its gleaming golden roofs and rich religious heritage.
Pudacuo National Park: Walk through the first national park in China to meet IUCN standards, encompassing alpine lakes, pastures, and forests — the very landscape that inspired Lost Horizon.

Shangri-La reveals its magic today. First, we visit Napa Lake, also known as Yila Prairie. This seasonal lake transforms into vast wetlands and grasslands depending on the time of year. Yak herds dot the landscape, and the reflection of blue sky in shallow water creates dreamlike scenes. Next, we tour Ganden Sumtseling Monastery, commonly called Songzanlin Monastery. Built in 1679, it's the largest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Yunnan, housing over 700 monks. Its design mimics the Potala Palace in Lhasa, earning it the nickname 'Little Potala.' The golden roofs gleam in the sunlight, and the chanting of monks fills the air with spiritual energy. After lunch, we enter Pudacuo National Park, China's first national park to meet international standards. The park encompasses Shudu Lake, where you can walk along boardwalks through primeval forest, and Bita Hai Lake, surrounded by lush meadows. This is the landscape that inspired James Hilton—snow-capped peaks, blue lakes, broad grassy marshlands, and pristine forests where humans and nature exist in perfect harmony.

Day 9
Return to Kunming: Flower Market & Dianchi Lake
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Train to Kunming → Arrival pick-up → Haigeng Dam (Dianchi Lake view) → Dounan Flower Market → Nanping Street exploration → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Dounan Flower Market: Immerse yourself in Asia's largest fresh flower trading hub, where millions of blooms are auctioned daily. The sea of colors and fragrances is overwhelming in the best way.
Haigeng Dam: Feed the thousands of seagulls that migrate from Siberia each winter (late Oct to March) and enjoy panoramic views of Dianchi Lake.
Nanping Street: Experience Kunming's modern heart, where locals shop, dine, and socialize against a backdrop of both historic and contemporary architecture.

A morning train returns us to Kunming, the provincial capital known as the 'Spring City' for its year-round mild climate. Our first stop is Haigeng Dam along Dianchi Lake—Yunnan's largest lake. From October to March, this area becomes a sanctuary for thousands of black-headed gulls that migrate from Siberia. You can feed them by hand as they wheel and dive against the mountain backdrop. Then we visit Dounan Flower Market, China's largest fresh flower trading hub. The scale is astonishing—stalls overflow with roses, lilies, carnations, orchids, and countless other varieties, freshly delivered from Yunnan's flower farms. In the retail section, vendors wrap bouquets in newspaper with incredible speed and skill. Even if you're not buying, the atmosphere is vibrant and utterly unique. Evening brings us to Nanping Street, Kunming's bustling commercial heart. This pedestrian street offers shopping, dining, and people-watching. Try local snacks, browse modern shops, or simply soak in the urban energy before your final night in China.
