Furong Ancient Town, also known as Wangcun, is a well-preserved ancient Tujia town with a history of over 2,000 years. It is famous for its unique architectural style of stilted buildings and the 60-meter-high waterfall running through the town, which earns it the reputation of "the town hanging on the waterfall". In the evening, the waterfall is decorated with colorful lights, creating a stunning night view that is a must-see photo spot. You can also taste local specialties such as Liu Xiaoqing Rice Tofu, Tujia Bacon, and Pine Mushroom Stewed Native Chicken in the town.

Morning – Furong Ancient Town: Stroll bluestone lanes, explore Tusi Palace for Tujia chieftain heritage, and walk behind a thundering 60m waterfall. Pick up handcrafted souvenirs along the way.

Evening – Must-see: 7:00pm City Opening Drum, 7:50pm Spectacular Tower Lighting Ceremony.

Morning: Ride the world's longest alpine cableway (7,455m, 30 min) – watch the city melt into clouds. Step onto the cliffside glass plank walk at 1,400m+ (optional shoe covers). Feel the thrill! Then conquer Guigu Plank Road, Yunmeng Fairy Top (Tianmen's highest peak), and Tianmen Mountain Temple.

Afternoon: Take the tunnel escalator to Tianmen Cave – a 131.5m natural arch, the legendary "Gateway to Heaven". Descend via eco-bus or brave the 999-step Heavenly Staircase.

Tianmen Mountain, in Zhangjiajie, is best known for its 99-bend mountain road—carved into the cliffside in 1998—and the dramatic natural arch, Tianmen Cave, which has drawn pilgrims and climbers for over 1,800 years. You’ll ride the world’s longest cable car (7.4 km) up through mist-wrapped sandstone pillars, then walk the glass skywalk clinging to the eastern cliff face—cool underfoot, with sheer drops you can see right through. The summit feels quiet and ancient, especially at dawn, when the cave’s stone frame catches the first light and the valley below still holds pockets of cloud. It’s less about spectacle and more about the slow, steady rhythm of climbing, pausing, and taking in how the mountain has shaped both landscape and legend over centuries.
