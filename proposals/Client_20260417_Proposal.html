Day 01
Day 1
Xi'an Arrival

Xi'an Xianyang International Airport — Warm name-sign pick-up
Hotel — Rest and climate adjustment

Upon arrival at Xi'an Xianyang International Airport, a professional driver meets you with a name sign for a smooth transfer to your hotel in Xi'an. After check-in and rest, you enjoy a casual local dinner to acclimate to Xi'an’s climate and rhythm. The remainder of the day is free for relaxation and preparation for the journey ahead. You’ll arrive in Xi’an where the city’s 3,100-year history feels quietly present—not in grand pronouncements, but in the weight of the Ming-era city walls you’ll walk at dusk, their 14-kilometre circuit still intact and lit softly along the ramparts. Just beyond the south gate, the Muslim Quarter hums with the scent of cumin-roasted lamb skewers and freshly baked *youxuan* flatbreads, while shopkeepers hand-carve seals using Song-dynasty techniques you can try yourself. Your driver will meet you just outside arrivals—not in a chaotic curb-side scramble, but at a quiet, shaded lane reserved for pre-arranged transfers—and whisk you to a boutique hotel tucked inside a restored Qing-era courtyard, where the only sound at night is the distant chime of the Bell Tower. It’s a city that reveals itself slowly: first in texture, then in layers—stone, spice, ink, and centuries—all held with a calm, unhurried certainty.

Day 02
Day 2
Xi'an Historical & Cultural Tour
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Terracotta Army Museum — 'Eighth Wonder of the World', mausoleum of Qin Shi Huang
Handmade terracotta warrior activity — Craft your own souvenir under professional guidance
Huaqing Pool — Historic imperial hot spring garden, site of Emperor Xuanzong and Yang Guifei's romance
Tang Dynasty song and dance performance — Immersive revival of Tang prosperity with dumpling or palace banquet

In the morning, visit the Terracotta Army Museum—the 'Eighth Wonder of the World'—to witness the monumental sculptures reflecting Qin Dynasty military power and craftsmanship. Then participate in a hands-on terracotta warrior craft activity guided by professionals. In the afternoon, explore Huaqing Pool’s scenic imperial gardens and hot springs tied to Tang Dynasty romance. The evening features a spectacular Tang Dynasty song and dance performance followed by a themed dumpling or palace banquet.

Day 03
Day 3
Xi'an City Style Tour
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Xi'an City Wall — Best-preserved ancient city wall in China; cycle the full 13.7 km
Giant Wild Goose Pagoda — Tang Dynasty landmark built to house scriptures brought by Monk Xuanzang
Chang'an Twelve Hours — Immersive Tang Dynasty living scene with architecture, costumes, snacks, and activities
Datang Never-Sleeping City — Nighttime Tang-style street with performances, lights, and snacks

Begin the day cycling atop the Xi'an City Wall—China’s best-preserved ancient rampart—to absorb panoramic views and historical atmosphere. Next, visit the Giant Wild Goose Pagoda, climb its tiers for city vistas, and reflect on its Buddhist heritage. In the afternoon, immerse yourself in the ‘Chang’an Twelve Hours’ scenic spot, a living re-creation of Tang-era life. End the day dressed in Tang-style costume while strolling Datang Never-Sleeping City’s lantern-lit alleys, sampling snacks and enjoying street performances.

Day 04
Day 4
Xi'an City Tour & Transfer to Xining
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car, High-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Bell and Drum Towers — Ming Dynasty time-telling landmarks at Xi'an’s historic core
Huimin Street — Bustling snack street with Roujiamo, Liangpi, Paomo, Tanghulu
High-speed rail — Comfortable, scenic transfer from Xi'an to Xining

Start with visits to the iconic Bell and Drum Towers—Ming Dynasty timekeeping structures offering sweeping views of Xi’an’s old city. Then explore Huimin Street’s vibrant food culture, tasting regional specialties and browsing souvenirs. After lunch, depart via high-speed rail from Xi’an North Railway Station to Xining Station. Upon arrival, a driver transfers you to your hotel; dinner follows to aid acclimatization to Xining’s plateau climate.

Day 05
Day 5
Xining to Qinghai Lake & Transfer to Chaka
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Qinghai Lake Erlang Sword Scenic Area — Largest inland lake in China; 'Blue Gem of the Plateau'
Qinghai Lake cruise or lakeside walk — Sky-and-lake fusion, grassland vistas, grazing livestock
Chaka Town — Overnight base before Chaka Salt Lake visit

Depart Xining by bus for Qinghai Lake’s Erlang Sword Scenic Area—China’s largest inland lake, famed for its vivid blue waters and surrounding grasslands dotted with cattle and sheep. Enjoy lake views, optional cruise, or peaceful lakeside walking amid fresh air and plateau scenery. In the afternoon, continue by bus to Chaka Town (~2 hours), check in to your hotel, have dinner, and rest in preparation for tomorrow’s salt lake exploration.

Day 06
Day 6
Chaka Salt Lake Tour & Transfer to Golmud
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Chaka Salt Lake — 'Mirror of the Sky'; reflective salt beach and surreal sky-lake symmetry
Salt lake small train ride — Journey into the heart of the salt flats
Chaka Salt Lake souvenir collection — Gather natural salt crystals (with safety caution)

Spend the morning at Chaka Salt Lake—one of China’s most stunning salt lakes—where white salt beaches mirror the azure sky, creating the famed 'Mirror of the Sky' effect. Ride a small train across the expanse, step carefully on the salt surface, and collect salt crystals as mementos. In the afternoon, travel by bus to Golmud (~4 hours), crossing dramatic plateau Gobi terrain with expansive deserts and unique landforms. Check in, dine, and rest upon arrival.

Day 07
Day 7
Golmud to Qarhan Salt Lake & Transfer to Dunhuang
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Qarhan Salt Lake — Largest salt lake in China; vast salt marshes and naturally formed salt towers
Salt road walk & salt tower viewing — Observe geological formations and learn salt production history
Gobi Desert transit — Scenic 5-hour drive through northwestern desert landscapes

Travel from Golmud to Qarhan Salt Lake—the largest salt lake in China and one of the world’s biggest—renowned as the 'Salt Capital of China'. Walk its salt roads, examine sculptural salt towers formed by mineral precipitation, and admire crystal-clear waters reflecting the surroundings. Learn about local salt industry history and geology. In the afternoon, embark on a ~5-hour bus journey to Dunhuang, traversing stark Gobi Desert vistas en route. Arrive, check in, dine, and rest in anticipation of Dunhuang’s cultural treasures.

Day 08
Day 8
Dunhuang Cultural & Natural Tour
▾
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Mogao Grottoes — UNESCO World Heritage Site; 'Art Gallery in the Desert', millennia of Buddhist murals and sculptures
Mingsha Mountain and Crescent Moon Spring — Singing Sand Dunes and oasis spring amid desert landscape

Begin the day at the Mogao Grottoes, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and legendary 'Art Gallery in the Desert', housing over a thousand caves adorned with Buddhist art spanning ten centuries. Later, visit Mingsha Mountain and the miraculous Crescent Moon Spring—an oasis nestled within towering singing sand dunes—where you may experience camel riding or sandboarding. The contrast between ancient spiritual art and surreal desert ecology defines this unforgettable day.
