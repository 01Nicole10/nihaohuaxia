The Bund Walk(40 mins)
Huangpu River Cruise(50 mins)
Rockbund Art Museum(45 mins)

The Bund is best experienced at dusk, when the colonial-era façades—like the 1923 Customs House clock tower and the 1907 HSBC Building’s bronze lions—soften in golden light while Pudong’s skyscrapers begin to glow across the Huangpu River. It’s a walk of quiet contrasts: you’ll pass weathered stone steps where merchants once unloaded tea and silk, then pause beside the riverside promenade, where locals practice tai chi at dawn or sip jasmine tea at open-air cafés by late afternoon. The 1.5-kilometre stretch is entirely pedestrian-friendly, lined with restored architecture spanning Neo-Renaissance to Art Deco—and just a five-minute stroll from Nanjing Road’s historic shopping arcades. You don’t need to rush it; the rhythm here is slow, thoughtful, and deeply rooted in Shanghai’s layered history.
