Guangzhou | Arrival in the Southern Gateway

Welcome to Guangzhou, the vibrant capital of Guangdong province and one of China's most dynamic cities. After your international flight, your guide will meet you at the airport and escort you to your hotel. Today is dedicated to rest and recovery, allowing you to adjust to the local time.

Your China journey begins in Guangzhou, a city that has served as a major trading port for centuries. Known historically as Canton, it remains one of China's most cosmopolitan destinations. Upon arrival at Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, your private guide will greet you at the arrival hall and transfer you to your hotel. Day 2
Guangzhou | Heritage, Colonial Charm & Modern Skyline
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Chen Clan Academy → Yuexiu Park → Shamian Island → Lunch → Yongqingfang → Canton Tower → Pearl River Night Cruise → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Chen Clan Academy — Explore a stunning example of traditional Lingnan architecture, featuring intricate wood carvings, stone sculptures, and ceramic decorations. Built between 1890 and 1894, it served as a study hall for Chen family members preparing for imperial examinations .
Yuexiu Park — Visit Guangzhou's largest park, home to the iconic Five Rams Statue—the symbol of the city—and the ancient Zhenhai Tower.
Shamian Island — Stroll through a peaceful colonial-era enclave where European consulates, churches, and mansions from the 19th century stand amidst tree-lined boulevards.
Yongqingfang — Wander through a beautifully restored historical neighborhood where traditional Cantonese architecture meets modern art galleries, boutique shops, and local eateries.
Canton Tower — Gaze at China's tallest tower (604 meters), an architectural marvel with a distinctive twisted design. The evening light display transforms the city's skyline.
Pearl River Night Cruise — Glide along the Pearl River as illuminated bridges and gleaming skyscrapers reflect in the water, offering a magical perspective of Guangzhou after dark.

Begin your Guangzhou exploration at Chen Clan Academy, a magnificent complex that represents the pinnacle of traditional Lingnan artistry. The intricate carvings, sculptures, and ceramics displayed throughout the building showcase the craftsmanship of the late Qing Dynasty. Originally built as a study hall and ancestral temple, it now houses the Guangdong Folk Art Museum.

Next, visit Yuexiu Park, a sprawling green oasis in the heart of the city. The park's centerpiece is the Five Rams Statue, commemorating the legend of five immortals who rode into Guangzhou on rams carrying rice—a story that gave the city its nickname "City of Rams" (Yangcheng). Climb Zhenhai Tower, a five-story structure built in 1380, which now houses the Guangzhou Museum and offers panoramic views of the city.

After lunch, explore Shamian Island, a sandbar island that became a foreign enclave following the Opium Wars. Walking along its quiet streets feels like stepping back in time—colonial-era buildings, banyan trees draped with aerial roots, and a relaxed atmosphere make this a favorite spot for photographers and history enthusiasts alike.

Continue to Yongqingfang, a revitalized historical district nestled along the banks of the Liwan Lake. Unlike the colonial architecture of Shamian Island, Yongqingfang showcases traditional Cantonese "qilou" (arcade-style) buildings. The area has been thoughtfully restored, blending ancient charm with contemporary creativity—you'll find traditional herb shops alongside stylish cafes and old courtyard homes transformed into art galleries.

As dusk falls, make your way to Canton Tower, the centerpiece of Guangzhou's modern skyline. Standing at 604 meters, it is the tallest TV tower in China and the third tallest globally. Its slender, hourglass shape has earned it the nickname "Twisted Waist Beauty." Viewing it from below as night falls is a spectacle in itself—its LED facade displays an ever-changing symphony of colors.

The evening culminates with a Pearl River Night Cruise. Board a comfortable sightseeing boat and sail past some of Guangzhou's most iconic landmarks: the Canton Tower, the modern architecture of Zhujiang New Town, historic buildings along the riverbanks, and several illuminated bridges. The reflections of city lights on the water create a romantic and unforgettable urban scene.

Guangzhou to Zhangjiajie | Journey to the Avatar Mountains
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Transfer to Guangzhou South Railway Station → High-speed train to Zhangjiajie West Station (approx. 6-7 hours) → Station pick-up → Hotel check-in → Rest
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Experience China's world-class high-speed rail network on a scenic journey from the urban metropolis of Guangzhou to the dramatic landscapes of Zhangjiajie. The comfortable, punctual trains offer a relaxing way to travel between cities while enjoying the changing scenery.

After breakfast and hotel check-out, your guide will accompany you to Guangzhou South Railway Station, one of China's busiest and most modern rail hubs. You'll board a high-speed train bound for Zhangjiajie West Station, a journey of approximately 6-7 hours.

China's high-speed rail system is renowned for its punctuality, comfort, and efficiency. Trains are equipped with spacious seats, power outlets, and a dining car serving snacks and meals. As you travel north, watch the landscape transform from the dense urban fabric of the Pearl River Delta to the lush, karst-studded hills of Hunan province.

Upon arrival in Zhangjiajie, your local guide will meet you at the station and escort you to your hotel. The rest of the day is yours to rest and prepare for the breathtaking natural wonders awaiting you.

Day 4
Zhangjiajie | Yuanjiajie, Tianzi Mountain & Ten-Mile Gallery
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Wulingyuan Scenic Area → Shuttle bus → Tianzi Mountain Cable Car → Tianzi Mountain exploration → Shuttle bus → Yuanjiajie exploration (Avatar Hallelujah Mountain, First Bridge Under Heaven) → Bailong Elevator → Shuttle bus to Ten-Mile Gallery → Optional sightseeing train → Return to hotel
✈ TransportShuttle bus, Cable car, Elevator
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Avatar Hallelujah Mountain — Stand before the 1,074-meter pillar that inspired the floating mountains in James Cameron's Avatar. The Southern Sky Column was officially renamed "Hallelujah Mountain" in 2010 following the film's global success .
Tianzi Mountain — Known as the "King of Peak Forests," this area offers panoramic views of thousands of stone pillars rising from the mist. The name commemorates a peasant rebel who declared himself "Son of Heaven" (Tianzi) in the 14th century .
Bailong Elevator — Ride the world's tallest outdoor elevator (326 meters), built directly into a cliff face. The 66-second ascent offers spectacular views as you emerge from the rock.
Ten-Mile Gallery — Admire the natural rock formations lining a picturesque valley, best viewed from the sightseeing train.

Today explores the heart of the Wulingyuan Scenic Area, a UNESCO World Heritage site and China's first national forest park. This protected landscape features over 3,000 quartz-sandstone pillars—some reaching heights of 800 meters—that inspired the scenery of Pandora in the film Avatar.

Begin at Tianzi Mountain, accessible via cable car. The area's name means "Son of Heaven Mountain," commemorating Xiang Dakun, a local farmer who led a peasant uprising in the 14th century and declared himself "Son of Heaven." From viewing platforms like He Long Park (dedicated to a Hunanese military leader) and West Sea, you'll gaze upon a seemingly endless array of stone pillars shrouded in mist. The Imperial Writing Brush Peak and Fairy Presenting Flowers are among the formations named for their resemblance to traditional Chinese motifs.

After lunch in the scenic area, transfer to Yuanjiajie, the section made internationally famous by Avatar. The highlight is Hallelujah Mountain (Southern Sky Column), a 1,074-meter pillar that served as the primary visual reference for the film's floating Hallelujah Mountains. Nearby, walk across the First Bridge Under Heaven, a natural stone arch connecting two peaks with a sheer drop of 350 meters beneath your feet. The Fascination Terrace offers multiple viewing platforms where pillars seem to float in a sea of green.

Descend via the Bailong Elevator, a remarkable engineering achievement that transports visitors from the heights of Yuanjiajie to the valley floor in just over a minute. As you exit, look back to appreciate the sheer scale of the cliff face you've just passed through.

Finally, visit Ten-Mile Gallery, where a series of peaks line a picturesque valley. The name is poetic rather than literal—the valley extends about 2.5 kilometers. You can take the sightseeing train (optional) to comfortably view formations named for their shapes: "Old Man Collecting Herbs," "Sisters Peak," and "Fingers of the Buddha." The gentle stroll or train ride provides a relaxing conclusion to a day of dramatic mountain scenery.

🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Tianmen Mountain Scenic Area → Cable car ascent → Glass Skywalk → Guigu Plank Road → Mountain-penetrating escalator → Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate) → 999 steps descent → Cable car down → Tujia Folk Customs Park → Transfer to Zhangjiajie West Station → High-speed train to Chongqing West Station (approx. 5 hours) → Station pick-up → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportCable car, Escalator, High-speed train
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Tianmen Mountain Cable Car — Experience one of the world's longest cable car rides (30 minutes), ascending 1,279 meters directly from the city center to the mountain summit .
Glass Skywalk — Challenge your courage on the 60-meter glass-bottomed walkway suspended on a sheer cliff face. Walking on transparent panels with the valley far below is an exhilarating test of nerve.
Heaven's Gate (Tianmen Cave) — Witness the world's highest natural karst arch, a massive hole 131.5 meters high and 57 meters wide punched through the mountain. Descend the 999 steps—a number symbolizing eternity in Chinese culture.
Tujia Folk Customs Park — Explore the cultural heritage of the Tujia ethnic group, learning about their traditional architecture, customs, and the ancient Tusi chieftain system.

Today you explore Tianmen Mountain, Zhangjiajie's iconic city-side peak. The adventure begins with the Tianmen Mountain Cable Car—one of the world's longest, rising directly from the city center to the summit. During the 30-minute journey, you'll watch the urban landscape shrink beneath you as you ascend into the clouds, with the famous 99-bend Tongtian Avenue (Road to Heaven) winding like a silver ribbon far below.

At the summit, follow the West Line trail. The Glass Skywalk is a 60-meter walkway suspended against a vertical cliff face with a transparent floor revealing the valley far below. Continue along the Guigu Plank Road, which hugs the mountain's contours, named after the legendary Guigu Cave where ancient strategists were said to have studied.

A series of mountain-penetrating escalators—a remarkable engineering achievement—carry you down through the mountain's interior. You'll emerge at Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate), a natural arch 131.5 meters high and 57 meters wide. Chongqing | Mountain City Heart & Hongyadong Night View
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Jiefangbei (Liberation Monument) → Bayi Food Street → Kuixinglou → Hongyadong night view → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotelnot mentioned
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Jiefangbei — Visit the symbolic heart of Chongqing, the Liberation Monument, surrounded by towering skyscrapers and bustling commercial streets.
Bayi Food Street — Dive into Chongqing's legendary food scene on this bustling pedestrian street, where you can sample authentic hot pot, spicy noodles, and countless local snacks.
Kuixinglou — Discover a restored historical complex that offers insight into Chongqing's traditional architecture and local culture.
Hongyadong Night View — Experience Chongqing's most iconic sight: a multi-level complex of traditional stilted houses built into a cliffside, illuminated with golden lights that cascade down the slope and reflect in the Jialing River.

Begin your Chongqing exploration at Jiefangbei (Liberation Monument), the symbolic center of Chongqing. Originally built as a monument to China's victory over Japan in World War II (when Chongqing served as the wartime capital), it was later renamed to commemorate the Communist liberation. Today, the monument stands at the heart of a dizzying commercial district surrounded by skyscrapers, luxury shops, and neon signs—a perfect encapsulation of Chongqing's vertical, high-energy character.

From Jiefangbei, walk to Bayi Food Street, the city's most famous culinary destination. This pedestrianized lane is packed with restaurants and food stalls serving Chongqing's legendary cuisine. The city is the birthplace of hot pot, and Bayi Street offers everything from traditional spicy hot pot to more approachable street snacks like spicy rabbit head, dan dan noodles, and chuan chuan (skewers). Even if you're not ready for extreme spice, you'll find plenty of milder options—the atmosphere alone is worth the visit.

Continue to Kuixinglou, a recently restored historical complex that offers a quieter glimpse into Chongqing's past. Named after the traditional Chinese deity of literature, this area features traditional courtyard architecture, winding alleyways, and exhibits on local culture. It's a quieter alternative to the city's more commercial areas and provides excellent photo opportunities.

As dusk falls, experience the magic of Hongyadong, Chongqing's most iconic attraction. This remarkable complex of traditional diaojiaolou (stilted houses) is built against a steep cliff overlooking the Jialing River. By day, it's an interesting architectural site; by night, it transforms into a spectacular display of golden lights that seem to cascade down the cliff face. The complex contains shops, restaurants, and teahouses spread across multiple levels, connected by winding staircases and narrow corridors. The illuminated buildings reflected in the river below create a magical scene that has become the defining image of Chongqing.

Chongqing | Urban Wonders & Train to Chengdu
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Check-out → Liziba Light Rail Station → Eling No.2 Factory → Ciqikou Ancient Town → Transfer to Chongqing West Station → High-speed train to Chengdu East Station (approx. 1.5-2 hours) → Station pick-up → Hotel check-in
✈ TransportHigh-speed train
🏨 Hotelnot mentioned
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Liziba Light Rail Station — Witness one of Chongqing's most famous urban oddities—a light rail train that passes directly through a residential building.
Eling No.2 Factory — Explore a repurposed industrial site transformed into a creative hub, offering art galleries, trendy cafes, and panoramic views of the Yangtze River.
Ciqikou Ancient Town — Step back in time at this well-preserved riverside town, where traditional architecture, local crafts, and street food evoke the atmosphere of old Chongqing.

Chongqing's unique topography has produced some extraordinary urban solutions, and Liziba Light Rail Station is perhaps the most famous. Here, Line 2 of the city's light rail system runs directly through a residential building—not above it or beside it, but through the middle. The building was constructed around the rail line, creating a seamless integration of transportation and housing that exemplifies Chongqing's creative approach to limited space. Watch the train arrive and depart, then consider how this unconventional arrangement functions in daily life.

Next, visit Eling No.2 Factory, a former printing factory transformed into a vibrant cultural and creative zone. This revitalized industrial complex now houses art galleries, designer studios, craft shops, and fashionable cafes. The site offers excellent views of the Yangtze River and the surrounding cityscape, and its industrial-chic aesthetic makes it a favorite among younger travelers and photographers.

In the afternoon, explore Ciqikou Ancient Town, a riverside settlement with over 1,000 years of history. Located along the Jialing River, Ciqikou was once a bustling commercial port. Today, its stone-paved streets, traditional courtyard homes, and historic temples have been preserved. Wander through the maze-like lanes, sample local specialties like Chen's Mahua (a type of fried dough twist) and various bean-based snacks, and browse shops selling traditional crafts, teas, and souvenirs.

After your exploration, transfer to Chongqing West Station for a high-speed train to Chengdu. The journey takes approximately 1.5-2 hours. Upon arrival at Chengdu East Station, your local guide will meet you and escort you to your hotel.

Chengdu | Pandas & Ancient Shu Civilization
▾
🗺Hotel breakfast → Chengdu Panda Base → Lunch → Sanxingdui Museum → Return to hotel
✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotelnot mentioned
🍽 MealsBreakfast, Lunch
Chengdu Panda Base — Observe giant pandas in a carefully designed naturalistic habitat at this world-renowned research and conservation facility. See pandas of all ages, from tiny cubs to adults, as they eat, play, and rest .
Sanxingdui Museum — Journey back 3,000 years to explore the mysterious Bronze Age civilization of the ancient Shu kingdom, featuring extraordinary artifacts—including towering bronze masks and golden scepters—that challenge conventional understanding of Chinese civilization .

Today focuses on two of Chengdu's most remarkable attractions, spanning thousands of years of history and culture.

Begin at the Chengdu Panda Base, a research and conservation facility dedicated to the protection of giant pandas and red pandas. Unlike a traditional zoo, the panda base provides a naturalistic environment where pandas roam in spacious enclosures with bamboo forests, climbing structures, and pools. The best viewing times are in the morning (around 8:00-10:00 AM) when the pandas are most active—eating bamboo, playing with each other, and occasionally wrestling. You'll see pandas of all ages, including adorable cubs in the nursery (seasonal). The facility also offers educational exhibits on conservation efforts to protect these beloved animals, whose wild population has slowly recovered thanks to dedicated work.

After lunch, venture to Sanxingdui Museum, one of China's most significant archaeological sites. Discovered in 1986, Sanxingdui revealed a previously unknown Bronze Age civilization that flourished in the Sichuan basin over 3,000 years ago, contemporaneous with the Shang dynasty of central China but with a completely distinct culture. The artifacts are astonishing: giant bronze masks with protruding eyes, towering bronze trees, gold scepters, and jade objects that bear no resemblance to any other Chinese civilization. These discoveries have led scholars to reconsider narratives about the origins of Chinese civilization. The museum's modern building and thoughtfully presented exhibits make it an unforgettable journey into a lost world.

Chengdu | Three Kingdoms, Teahouse Culture & Modern City
▾

✈ TransportPrivate car
🏨 Hotelnot mentioned
🍽 MealsBreakfast
Wuhou Shrine — Pay homage to Zhuge Liang, the legendary strategist of the Three Kingdoms period, at this beautifully preserved temple complex dedicated to him and his contemporaries.
People's Park & Heming Teahouse — Experience everyday Chengdu life at this beloved urban oasis, where locals practice tai chi, sing traditional songs, and gather at the famous Heming Teahouse for tea and mahjong .
Chunxi Road — Experience Chengdu's premier shopping district, a bustling pedestrianized street lined with international brands, department stores, and local shops.
Taikoo Li — Explore a sophisticated open-air shopping complex that artfully blends contemporary architecture with preserved historic buildings.

Begin at Wuhou Shrine, a temple complex dedicated to Zhuge Liang (Kongming), the brilliant military strategist of the Three Kingdoms period (220-280 AD). While originally built to honor him, the shrine also commemorates Liu Bei, the emperor of the Shu Han kingdom, and other heroes of this legendary era. The complex features classical Chinese architecture, ancient cypress trees, and numerous statues and steles. The stories of Zhuge Liang's wisdom, loyalty, and strategic brilliance have been celebrated in Chinese culture for nearly 2,000 years, and visiting his shrine offers insight into one of China's most beloved historical periods.

Continue to People's Park (Renmin Gongyuan), the green heart of Chengdu and a window into local life. Unlike tourist-oriented attractions, People's Park is where Chengdu residents come to relax, socialize, and pursue their hobbies. You'll see elderly people practicing tai chi, groups singing traditional opera, mahjong players gathered under trees, and couples ballroom dancing in open pavilions. The park is also home to the historic Heming Teahouse, where you can experience Chengdu's famous teahouse culture—order a cup of jasmine tea, find a bamboo chair, and watch the world go by. This is the quintessential Chengdu experience: relaxed, sociable, and deeply connected to the city's identity.

In the afternoon, experience Chengdu's modern urban energy. Chunxi Road is Chengdu's premier shopping destination—a bustling pedestrianized street packed with flagship stores, department stores, and a constant flow of fashionably dressed locals. The energy here is infectious.
